Barry Town United v Newtown

Cymru Premier | Jenner Park
Saturday, 16 December 2023 @ 2.30pm

Relegation candidates Barry Town United face a challenge against high-flying Newtown at Jenner Park on Saturday.

Barry Town United are ninth in the Cymru Premier table, and have picked up four wins and four draws in their 18 games to date.

The White Stars, meanwhile, are third in the standings with 33 points, having won 10 and drawn three, and are 11 points behind table-toppers The New Saints.

With 19 goals in 18 games so far this season, Barry Town are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.1 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 37 goals at a rate of 2.1 per game.

Newtown, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.8 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.1 goals per game.

In Aaron Williams, the White Stars have one of the league's sharpest shooters so far this season. He has notched 12 goals in 18 appearances, to sit second in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 131 minutes is quicker than that of Ollie Hulbert, United's top scorer with a goal every 199 minutes, and a total of eight goals in 18 games.

In the last five years, Barry Town and Newtown have played each other on 10 occasions. Barry Town won one of them, Newtown seven, and they drew twice.

On average, the Dragons scored 0.6 goals and the White Stars 1.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 16, when Newtown won 2-0 at home.

The Dragons are in mixed form in the Cymru Premier, with two wins and a draw from their last six games.

With three wins and two draws over that period, the visitors' form is better – they have taken 11 points from 18, compared to the home team's seven.

Barry Town's last match was on December 2, a 7-0 loss against Connah's Quay.

Newtown beat Colwyn Bay 4-2 last time out, also on December 2, with Williams (two), Louis Gabriel Robles and Ryan Sears on the scoresheet.
