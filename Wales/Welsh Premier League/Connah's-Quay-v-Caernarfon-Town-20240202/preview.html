Connah's Quay v Caernarfon Town

Welsh Premier League | Essity Stadium
Friday, 2 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Friday's late match between Connah's Quay and Caernarfon Town promises to be one for the neutrals, as two of Welsh Premier League's most free-scoring sides go head-to-head.

Ahead of the game at the Essity Stadium, the Nomads and the Canaries sit second and third in the goal-scoring charts, with 59 and 40 goals respectively.

Striker Jordan Davies leads the way for the home side, having bagged 15 goals in their 22 games to date.

And Adam Davies has been the main man in the opponents' penalty box for Caernarfon, with 12 goals.

Connah's Quay are second in the table after 22 games, of which they have won 15 and drawn two, earning 47 points.

Caernarfon are four places behind the Nomads in sixth, with nine wins and four draws putting them on 31 points.

In the last 10 years, Connah's Quay and Caernarfon have played each other on 18 occasions. Connah's Quay won 12 of them, Caernarfon two, and they drew four times.

On average, the Nomads scored 2.8 goals and the Canaries 0.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 27, when Connah's Quay won 6-1 at home.

The hosts are in fantastic form in Welsh Premier League, with five wins and one loss from their last six games.

With two wins and two draws over that period, Town's form is much worse – they have taken eight points from 18, compared to Connah's Quay's 15.

Connah's Quay's last match was on January 13, a 1-0 win against Penybont, with Michael Wilde getting the goal for the Nomads.

Caernarfon drew 2-2 with Cardiff Met last time out, also on January 13, with Adam Davies and Danny Gosset on the scoresheet.
