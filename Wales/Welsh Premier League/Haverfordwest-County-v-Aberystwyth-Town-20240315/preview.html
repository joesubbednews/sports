Haverfordwest County v Aberystwyth Town

Welsh Premier League | New Bridge Meadow Stadium
Friday, 15 March 2024 @ 7.45pm

Haverfordwest County host Aberystwyth Town in Friday's late match at the New Bridge Meadow Stadium looking to bounce back from defeat last time out in the Welsh Premier League.

The Bluebirds, who sit zero in the league after 27 games, fell to a 1-0 home defeat to Penybont on March 8.

They face an Aberystwyth side who secured a draw in their last match, a 1-1 tie with Barry Town United, and who sit zero in the table.

With 21 goals in 26 games so far this season, Aberystwyth are the league's-5th-lowest scorers with 0.8 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 49 goals at a rate of 1.9 per game.

Haverfordwest are also below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.4 goals per game.

County are in bad form in the Welsh Premier League, with no wins and three draws from their last six games.

With two wins and two draws over that period, Town's form is better – they have taken eight points from 18, compared to the Bluebirds' three.

In the last 10 years, Haverfordwest and Aberystwyth have played each other on 19 occasions. Haverfordwest won seven of them, Aberystwyth eight, and they drew four times.

On average, the Bluebirds scored 1.4 goals and the Seasiders 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on February 2, when Aberystwyth won 1-0 at home.
