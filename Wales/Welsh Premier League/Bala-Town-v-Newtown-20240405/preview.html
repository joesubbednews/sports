Bala Town v Newtown

Welsh Premier League | Maes Tegid
Friday, 5 April 2024 @ 7.45pm

Bala Town host Newtown in Friday's late match at Maes Tegid looking to bounce back from defeat last time out in the Welsh Premier League.

The Lakesiders, who sit third in the league after 28 games, fell to a 0-1 away defeat to Cardiff Met on March 16.

They face a Newtown side who secured a draw in their last match, a 0-0 tie with Connah's Quay, and who sit zero in the table.

In the last 10 years, Bala and Newtown have played each other on 35 occasions. Bala won 22 of them, Newtown seven, and they drew six times.

On average, the Lakesiders scored 2.0 goals and the White Stars 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on February 24, when Bala won 5-1 away.

Bala are in reasonable form in the Welsh Premier League, with three wins and two draws from their last six games.

With a win and a draw over that period, Newtown's form is much worse – they have taken four points from 18, compared to Town's 11.

With 34 goals in 28 games so far this season, the Lakesiders are the league's joint-lowest scorers with 1.2 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 24 goals at a rate of 0.9 per game.

The White Stars are also below average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.
