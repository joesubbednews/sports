The New Saints v Newtown

Welsh Premier League | Park Hall Stadium
Tuesday, 6 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Tuesday's late match between The New Saints and Newtown promises to be one for the neutrals, as two of Welsh Premier League's most free-scoring sides go head-to-head.

Ahead of the game at the Park Hall Stadium, the Saints and the White Stars sit top and fourth in the goal-scoring charts, with 74 and 34 goals respectively.

Midfielder Brad Young leads the way for the home side, having bagged 14 goals in their 21 games to date.

And Aaron Williams has been the main man in the opponents' penalty box for Newtown, with 13 goals.

The New Saints are top of the table after 21 games, of which they have won 19 and drawn two, earning 59 points.

Newtown are four places behind the Saints in fifth, with 10 wins and three draws putting them on 33 points.

The hosts are in exceptional form in Welsh Premier League, with six wins and no losses from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, the White Stars's form is much worse – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to The New Saints' 18.

In the last 10 years, The New Saints and Newtown have played each other on 34 occasions. The New Saints won 24 of them, Newtown four, and they drew six times.

On average, the Saints scored 2.8 goals and the White Stars 0.9 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 4, when The New Saints won 2-0 away.

The New Saints' last match was on January 6, a 8-0 win against Cardiff Met, with Young (three), Ryan Brobbel (two), Adrian Cieślewicz, Declan McManus and Rory Holden getting the goals for the Saints.

Newtown lost 4-0 against Barry Town United last time out, also on January 6.
