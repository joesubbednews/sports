Barry Town United v Penybont

Welsh Premier League | Jenner Park
Friday, 29 March 2024 @ 12.30pm

Barry Town United host Penybont in Friday lunchtime's match at Jenner Park looking to bounce back from defeat last time out in the Welsh Premier League.

The Dragons, who sit zero in the league after 28 games, fell to a 1-1 away defeat to Pontypridd Town AFC on March 16.

They face a Penybont side who picked up a win in their last match, a 5-0 victory against Colwyn Bay, and who sit fifth in the table.

In the last 10 years, Barry Town and Penybont have played each other on 11 occasions. Barry Town won two of them, Penybont five, and they drew four times.

On average, the Dragons scored 1.0 goal and Bont 1.5 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on February 10, when they played out a 2-2 draw.

Barry Town are in disappointing form in the Welsh Premier League, with no wins and five draws from their last six games.

With three wins and two draws over that period, Penybont's form is much better – they have taken 11 points from 18, compared to United's five.

With 33 goals in 28 games so far this season, the Dragons are the league's joint--1th-lowest scorers with 1.2 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 50 goals at a rate of 1.8 per game.

Bont are also below average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.3 goals per game.
