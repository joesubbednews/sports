Dunfermline Athletic v Queen's Park

Championship | KDM Group East End Park
Saturday, 20 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Struggling Queen's Park face Dunfermline Athletic away at KDM Group East End Park on Saturday looking to build on a win in their last league outing.

After securing all three points with a 2-1 victory over Raith Rovers on January 13, the Spiders sit ninth in the Championship.

They travel to play a Dunfermline side sixth in the standings, who lost in their last match, 2-1 against Queen's Park, on January 5.

In Ruari Paton, Queen's Park have the league's most on-form striker so far this season. He has notched 10 goals in 20 appearances.

His goal rate of one every 179 minutes is quicker than that of Craig Wighton, Dunfermline's top scorer with a goal every 207 minutes, and a total of five goals in 17 games.

With 24 goals in 19 games so far this season, Athletic are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.3 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 24 goals at a rate of 1.3 per game.

The Spiders are also below average scorers, with 1.3 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.4. They have conceded 2.0 goals per game.

The home team are in disappointing form in the Championship, with one win and two draws from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, the visitors' form is slightly better – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to Dunfermline's five.

In the last five years, Dunfermline and Queen's Park have played each other on four occasions. Dunfermline won one of them, Queen's Park two, and they drew once.

On average, Dunfermline scored 0.8 goals and the Spiders 0.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on January 5, when Queen's Park won 2-1 at home.

Saturday's match will be refereed by Iain Snedden, who has taken charge of six Championship games so far this season, issuing one red card and booking 27 players. He has awarded two penalties.

The last Dunfermline game Snedden refereed was a 1-0 away loss to Raith Rovers on October 28. He is yet to oversee a match featuring Queen's Park this season.
