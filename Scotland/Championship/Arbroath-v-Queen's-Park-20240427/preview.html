Arbroath v Queen's Park

Championship | Gayfield Park
Saturday, 27 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Saturday's match at Gayfield Park sees the league's two bottom sides face each other, as rock-bottom Arbroath host Queen's Park.

Arbroath have picked up 23 points from their first 23 Championship games, with six wins and five draws.

That is 14 points less than the Spiders have collected, having won nine and drawn 10.

Arbroath are in awful form in the Championship, with no wins and six losses from their last six games.

With a win and two draws over that period, Queen's Park's form is better – they have taken five points from 18, compared to the hosts' zero.

In the last 10 years, Arbroath and Queen's Park have played each other on 19 occasions. Arbroath won five of them, Queen's Park 12, and they drew twice.

On average, Arbroath scored 0.9 goals and the Spiders 1.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on February 27, when Queen's Park won 6-0 at home.

In Ruari Paton, the Spiders have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 16 goals in 34 appearances, to sit third in the scoring charts.

Arbroath's top scorers, with six goals each, are Jermaine Hylton and Jay Bird.

With 35 goals in 34 games so far this season, Arbroath are the league's lowest scorers with 1.0 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 79 goals at a rate of 2.3 per game.

The away team are also below average scorers, with 1.3 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.4. They have conceded 1.6 goals per game.

Arbroath's last match was on April 20, a 2-1 loss against Greenock Morton, with Michael McKenna getting the goal for Arbroath.

Queen's Park drew 0-0 with Dunfermline Athletic last time out, also on April 20.
