Nairn County v Banks O' Dee

Highland and Lowland Football Leagues | Station Park
Saturday, 16 December 2023 @ 3.00pm

Nairn County host Banks O' Dee at Station Park on Saturday on the back of seven consecutive wins in Highland and Lowland Football Leagues.

It means Nairn have picked up the maximum 21 points from their last seven games, and they face a Banks O' Dee side who have also won their last four matches, and collected 19 points from the last possible 21.

With 42 goals in 16 games so far this season, Banks O' Dee are scoring more than average in the league with 2.6 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 14 goals at a rate of 0.9 per game.

Nairn are also above average scorers, with 1.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

The visitors are fourth in the table after 16 games, of which they have won 10 and drawn five, earning 35 points.

County are five places behind Banks O' Dee in ninth, with 10 wins and five losses putting them on 30 points.

Over the last year, Nairn and Banks O' Dee have played each other twice. They won one each.

Their last meeting was on August 5, when Banks O' Dee won 3-0 at home.

Nairn's last match was on November 25, a 3-2 win against Wick Academy, with Aaron Nicolson, George Robesten and Jamie Chris Carnihan getting the goals for Nairn.

Banks O' Dee beat Strathspey Thistle 4-1 last time out, on December 2, with Dajon Golding, Lachlan MacLeod, Max Alexander and Michael Philipson on the scoresheet.
