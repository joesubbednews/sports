Hearts U21 v East Stirlingshire

Lowland Football League | Ainslie Park Stadium
Friday, 5 April 2024 @ 7.45pm

Hearts U21 are heavy favourites to keep all three points at home in Friday's late kick-off against East Stirlingshire.

Hearts, who sit second in the Lowland Football League with 31 games played, are priced at 1.2 to seal victory at the Ainslie Park Stadium.

Sitting 12 places and 28 points behind them in the table, East Stirlingshire are 8.5 to win with *Betting Company*, while the draw is at 5.8.

Hearts U21 are in exceptional form in the Lowland Football League, with six wins and no losses from their last six games.

With five wins and one loss over that period, East Stirlingshire's form is worse – they have taken 15 points from 18, compared to Hearts's 18.

With 82 goals in 31 games so far this season, the hosts are the league's second-highest scorers with 2.6 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 36 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

The Shire, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.5 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 1.8 goals per game.

In the last three years, Hearts U21 and East Stirlingshire have played each other on three occasions. Hearts U21 won two of them and they drew once.

Their last meeting was on September 29, when they played out a 1-1 draw.

Hearts U21's last match was on March 23, a 3-0 win against Gretna 2008.

East Stirlingshire beat Cowdenbeath 2-1 last time out, also on March 23.
