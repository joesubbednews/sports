Glasgow City Women v Celtic Women

SWPL 1 | Petershill Park
Sunday, 28 January 2024 @ 2.010pm

Two of SWPL 1's top sides face each other at Petershill Park in Sunday's early kick-off, when third-placed Glasgow City Women host second-placed Celtic Women.

Glasgow City have picked up 13 wins and two draws from 17 games so far this season, and sit five points below the visitors going into the 2.010pm match.

Celtic, meanwhile, have won 15 and drawn one, picking up 46 points.

With 90 goals in 17 games so far this season, Celtic are the league's highest scorers with 5.3 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in nine goals at a rate of 0.5 per game.

Glasgow City are also above average scorers, with 2.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 2.2. They have conceded 0.6 goals per game.

In Amy Gallagher, the away side have the league's most on-form striker so far this season. She has notched 18 goals in 16 appearances.

Her goal rate of one every 76 minutes is quicker than that of Lauren Davidson, the hosts' top scorer with a goal every 124 minutes, and a total of 10 goals in 17 games.

Glasgow City are in fantastic form in SWPL 1, with five wins and one loss from their last six games.

But with five wins and a draw over that period, Celtic's form is even better – they have taken 16 points from 18, compared to Glasgow City's 15.

In the last 10 years, Glasgow City and Celtic have played each other on 13 occasions. Glasgow City won six of them, Celtic five, and they drew twice.

On average, Glasgow City scored 1.4 goals and Celtic 1.3 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 10, when Celtic won 3-0 at home.

Glasgow City's last match was on January 14, a 5-1 win against Spartans Women, with Davidson (two), Aleigh Gambone, Emily Whelan and Kinga Kozak getting the goals for Glasgow City.

Celtic beat Montrose Women 9-0 last time out, also on January 14, with Gallagher (two), Tiree Burchill (two), Colette Cavanagh, Kelly Clark, Menglu Shen, Murphy Agnew and Sydney Cummings on the scoresheet.
