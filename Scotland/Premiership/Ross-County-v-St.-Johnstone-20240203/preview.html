Ross County v St. Johnstone

Premiership | Global Energy Stadium
Saturday, 3 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Saturday's match at the Global Energy Stadium sees two relegation candidates play each other, as 11th-ranked Ross County host 10th-placed St. Johnstone.

Ross have picked up 19 points from their first 19 Premiership games, with four wins and seven draws.

That is two points less than the Saints have collected, having won four and drawn nine.

Ross are in terrible form in Premiership, with no wins and two draws from their last six games.

With a win and three draws over that period, St. Johnstone's form is better – they have taken six points from 18, compared to County's two.

In the last 10 years, Ross and St. Johnstone have played each other on 31 occasions. Ross won nine of them, St. Johnstone 10, and they drew 12 times.

On average, the Staggies scored 1.2 goals and the Saints 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 11, when St. Johnstone won 1-0 at home.

With 15 goals in 22 games so far this season, the Saints are the league's joint-second-lowest scorers with 0.7 goals per game. And they are conceding at an average rate, letting in 29 goals at a rate of 1.3 per game.

The Staggies are also below average scorers, with 0.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

In Simon Murray, the home team have one of the league's sharpest shooters so far this season. He has notched six goals in 21 appearances, to sit ninth in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 258 minutes is slightly quicker than that of Nicholas Clark, the visitors' top scorer with a goal every 101 minutes, and a total of four goals in seven games.

Ross's last match was on Tuesday, a 2-2 draw against Livingston, with Murray getting the goals for the Staggies.

St. Johnstone drew 1-1 with Motherwell last time out, on January 27, with Ryan McGowan on the scoresheet.

Saturday's match will be refereed by Craig Napier, who has taken charge of three Premiership games so far this season, issuing no red cards and booking 11 players. He has awarded two penalties.

He is yet to oversee a match featuring either Ross or St. Johnstone this season.
