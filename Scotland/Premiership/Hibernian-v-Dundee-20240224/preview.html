Hibernian v Dundee

Premiership | Easter Road Stadium
Saturday, 24 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Hibernian face Dundee at the Easter Road Stadium on Saturday looking to secure a first win in nine Premiership games.

Hibs have lost five and drawn three matches since they last earned three points – against Livingston on December 9.

They face a Dundee side who have won three and drawn two over that time.

With 35 goals in 25 games so far this season, Dundee are the league's joint-third-highest scorers with 1.4 goals per game. But they are conceding more than average too, letting in 39 goals at a rate of 1.6 per game.

Hibernian, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.2 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.6 goals per game.

In the last 10 years, Hibernian and Dundee have played each other on 14 occasions. Hibernian won eight of them, Dundee one, and they drew five times.

On average, Hibs scored 1.7 goals and the Dees 0.9 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 25, when Hibernian won 2-1 away.

Hibs are eighth in the table after 25 games, of which they have won six and drawn nine, earning 27 points.

The Dees are two places ahead of the hosts in sixth, with eight wins and eight draws putting them on 32 points.

Hibernian's last match was on February 17, a 2-2 draw against Aberdeen, with Emiliano Marcondes and Martin Boyle getting the goals for Hibs.

Dundee beat Ross County 2-0 last time out, also on February 17, with Scott Tiffoney on the scoresheet.
