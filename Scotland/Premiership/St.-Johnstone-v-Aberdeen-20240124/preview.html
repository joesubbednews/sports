St. Johnstone v Aberdeen

Premiership | McDiarmid Park
Wednesday, 24 January 2024 @ 7.45pm

St. Johnstone host Aberdeen on Wednesday at McDiarmid Park in Premiership.

In their last league match, on December 27, St. Johnstone drew with Livingston 0-0 away.

Aberdeen won, 3-0 away at Ross County on January 2, with Jamie McGrath (two) and Bojan Miovski on the scoresheet.

In the last 10 years, St. Johnstone and Aberdeen have played each other on 37 occasions. St. Johnstone won nine of them, Aberdeen 16, and they drew 12 times.

On average, the Saints scored 0.8 goals and Aberdeen 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 8, when they played out a 0-0 draw.

St. Johnstone are 10th in the table after 20 games, of which they have won four and drawn seven, earning 19 points.

Aberdeen are three places ahead of the Saints in seventh, with six wins and four draws putting them on 22 points.

In Miovski, the visitors have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched nine goals in 18 appearances, to sit fifth in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 170 minutes is slightly quicker than that of Nicholas Clark, the home team's top scorer with a goal every 94 minutes, and a total of four goals in six games.

With 13 goals in 20 games so far this season, St. Johnstone are the league's second-lowest scorers with 0.7 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 27 goals at a rate of 1.4 per game.

Aberdeen are also below average scorers, with 1.2 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.6 goals per game.

St. Johnstone are in mixed form in Premiership, with two wins and two draws from their last six games.

With three wins and three losses over that period, Aberdeen's form is slightly better – they have taken nine points from 18, compared to the Saints' eight.

Wednesday's match will be refereed by John Beaton, who has taken charge of 11 Premiership games so far this season, issuing one red card and booking 51 players. He has awarded 10 penalties.

The last St. Johnstone game Beaton refereed was a 2-1 away loss to Kilmarnock on December 23. His last Aberdeen match was their 2-2 draw away at St. Mirren on August 27.
