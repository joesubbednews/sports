Livingston v Ross County

Premiership | Tony Macaroni Arena
Saturday, 27 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Struggling Livingston face Ross County at the Tony Macaroni Arena on Saturday looking to build on a win in their last league outing.

After securing all three points with a 2-4 victory over Hearts on April 13, Livingston sit bottom of the Premiership.

They travel to play a Ross side 11th in the standings, who also won their last match, 3-2 against Rangers, on April 14.

Livingston are in terrible form in the Premiership, with no wins and two draws from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, Ross's form is better – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to the hosts' two.

In the last 10 years, Livingston and Ross have played each other on 18 occasions. Livingston won nine of them, Ross four, and they drew five times.

On average, Livingston scored 1.7 goals and the Staggies 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on February 24, when Ross won 3-2 at home.

In Simon Murray, County have one of the league's sharpest shooters so far this season. He has notched 11 goals in 32 appearances, to sit seventh in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 229 minutes is quicker than that of Bruce Anderson, Livingston's top scorer with a goal every 315 minutes, and a total of five goals in 30 games.

With 22 goals in 33 games so far this season, Livingston are the league's lowest scorers with 0.7 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 59 goals at a rate of 1.8 per game.

The Staggies are also below average scorers, with 1.0 goal per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.7 goals per game.
