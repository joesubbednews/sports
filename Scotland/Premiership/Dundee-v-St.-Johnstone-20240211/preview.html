Dundee v St. Johnstone

Premiership | Kilmac Stadium at Dens Park
Sunday, 11 February 2024 @ 2.00pm

St. Johnstone travel to Dundee on early Sunday in the Premiership.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a defeat in their last match, having lost to Hearts 1-0 at home.

The Dees also lost their last match, 2-0 against St. Mirren.

In the last 10 years, Dundee and St. Johnstone have played each other on 25 occasions. Dundee won eight of them, St. Johnstone 12, and they drew five times.

On average, the Dees scored 0.9 goals and the Saints 1.3 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 2, when they played out a 2-2 draw.

With 16 goals in 24 games so far this season, St. Johnstone are the league's second-lowest scorers with 0.7 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 30 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

Dundee, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.3 goals per game. They have conceded 1.7 goals per game.

The Saints are 10th in the table after 24 games, of which they have won five and drawn nine, earning 24 points.

The Dees are four places ahead of the away team in sixth, with six wins and eight draws putting them on 26 points.

The hosts are in disappointing form in the Premiership, with one win and two draws from their last six games.

With a win and three draws over that period, St. Johnstone's form is slightly better – they have taken six points from 18, compared to Dundee's five.

Sunday's match will be refereed by William Collum, who has taken charge of 12 Premiership games so far this season, issuing one red card and booking 37 players. He has awarded four penalties.

The last Dundee game Collum refereed was a 2-0 away win against Livingston on October 28. His last St. Johnstone match was their 2-2 draw at home against Dundee on September 2.
