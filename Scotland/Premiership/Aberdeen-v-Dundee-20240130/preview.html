Aberdeen v Dundee

Premiership | Pittodrie Stadium
Tuesday, 30 January 2024 @ 7.45pm

Dundee travel to Aberdeen on late Tuesday in Premiership.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a win in their last match, having beaten Livingston 4-1 away, with goals from Amadou Bakayoko, Luke McCowan, Zach Robinson and Michael Mellon.

Aberdeen, meanwhile, lost their last match, 2-0 against Hearts.

With 28 goals in 20 games so far this season, Dundee are scoring more than average in the league with 1.4 goals per game. But they are conceding more than average too, letting in 32 goals at a rate of 1.6 per game.

Aberdeen, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.6 goals per game.

In Bojan Miovski, the home side have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 10 goals in 20 appearances, to sit second in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 171 minutes is quicker than that of Bakayoko, the Dees's top scorer with a goal every 327 minutes, and a total of five goals in 20 games.

In the last 10 years, Aberdeen and Dundee have played each other on 21 occasions. Aberdeen won 16 of them, Dundee one, and they drew four times.

On average, Aberdeen scored 2.2 goals and the Dees 0.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on April 30 2022, when Aberdeen won 1-0 at home.

Aberdeen are in mixed form in Premiership, with three wins and a draw from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, the visitors' form is worse – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to Aberdeen's 10.

The hosts are eighth in the table after 20 games, of which they have won six and drawn five, earning 23 points.

Dundee are one place ahead of Aberdeen in seventh, with six wins and seven draws putting them on 25 points.

Tuesday's match will be refereed by Nick Walsh, who has taken charge of 11 Premiership games so far this season, issuing two red cards and booking 54 players. He has awarded four penalties.

The last Aberdeen game Walsh refereed was a 2-1 home win against Livingston on December 20. He is yet to oversee a match featuring Dundee this season.
