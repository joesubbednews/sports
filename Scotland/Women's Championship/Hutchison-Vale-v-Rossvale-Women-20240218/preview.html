Hutchison Vale v Rossvale Women

Women's Championship | Oriam National Performance Centre
Sunday, 18 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Relegation candidates Hutchison Vale face a challenge against high-flying Rossvale Women at Oriam National Performance Centre on Sunday.

Hutchison Vale are rooted to the bottom of the Women's Championship table, and have picked up no wins and no draws in their 11 games to date.

Rossvale, meanwhile, are top of the standings with 33 points, having won 11 and drawn none of their first 12 matches.

Vale are in awful form in the Women's Championship, with no wins and six losses from their last six games.

With five wins and one loss over that period, Rossvale's form is much better – they have taken 15 points from 18, compared to the home team's zero.

With three goals in 11 games so far this season, Vale are the league's second-lowest scorers with 0.3 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 84 goals at a rate of 7.6 per game.

The visitors, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 4.8 goals per game, compared to a league average of 2.7. They have conceded 1.1 goals per game.

In the last 10 years, Vale and Rossvale have played each other on seven occasions. Rossvale won all of them.

On average, Vale scored 1.0 goal and Rossvale 4.7 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 1, when Rossvale won 11-0 at home.

Vale's last match was on February 11, a 8-0 loss against Ayr United Women.
