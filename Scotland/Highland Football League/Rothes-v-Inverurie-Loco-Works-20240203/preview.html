Rothes v Inverurie Loco Works

Highland Football League | MacKessack Park
Saturday, 3 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Rothes face Inverurie Loco Works at MacKessack Park on Saturday looking to secure a first win in nine Highland Football League games.

Rothes have lost four and drawn four matches since they last earned three points – against Forres Mechanics on November 25.

They face an Inverurie Loco Works side who have won four and drawn one over that time.

In the last 10 years, Rothes and Inverurie Loco Works have played each other on 14 occasions. Rothes won three of them, Inverurie Loco Works nine, and they drew twice.

On average, Rothes scored 0.8 goals and Inverurie Loco Works 3.3 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 30, when Rothes won 1-0 away.

With 29 goals in 23 games so far this season, Rothes are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.3 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 39 goals at a rate of 1.7 per game.

Inverurie Loco Works are also below average scorers, with 1.5 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.9. They have conceded 2.1 goals per game.

The visitors are 11th in the table after 21 games, of which they have won six and drawn two, earning 20 points.

The home team are one place ahead of Inverurie Loco Works in 10th, with eight wins and five draws putting them on 29 points.

Rothes's last match was on Wednesday, a 2-2 draw against Strathspey Thistle.

Inverurie Loco Works beat Nairn County 6-0 last time out, on January 27.
