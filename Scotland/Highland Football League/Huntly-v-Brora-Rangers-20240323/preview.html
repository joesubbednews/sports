Huntly v Brora Rangers

Highland Football League | Christie Park
Saturday, 23 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Huntly face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form at home against a tight Brora Rangers defence on Saturday.

With 79 goals in 30 games, Huntly are the top scorers in the Highland Football League ahead of the 3pm kick-off at Christie Park.

They face a Brora side who have scored 54 in 26 matches, but conceded only 32 goals, putting them fourth among the league's tightest defences – only Brechin City, Banks O' Dee and Buckie Thistle have conceded fewer goals.

In the last 10 years, Huntly and Brora have played each other on 13 occasions. Huntly won one of them and Brora the other.

On average, Huntly scored 0.6 goals and Brora 3.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 18, when Brora won 3-0 at home.

Brora are sixth in the table after 26 games, of which they have won 15 and drawn seven, earning 52 points.

Huntly are one place behind Rangers in seventh, with 15 wins and five draws putting them on 50 points.

The hosts are in reasonable form in the Highland Football League, with three wins and two draws from their last six games.

With four wins and two losses over that period, the away team's form is slightly better – they have taken 12 points from 18, compared to Huntly's 11.

Huntly's last match was on Wednesday, a 1-1 draw against Keith.

Brora beat Clachnacuddin 1-0 last time out, also on Wednesday.
