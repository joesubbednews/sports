Keith v Nairn County

Highland Football League | Kynoch Park
Saturday, 10 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Keith welcome Nairn County to Kynoch Park on Saturday looking to pick up points to end their six-game losing streak.

Keith's struggles have left them with no points from their last six Highland Football League matches, while their opponents have earned 10 from a possible 18.

Keith are 17th in the table after 19 games, of which they have won three and drawn two, earning 11 points.

Nairn are 12 places ahead of the hosts in fifth, with 13 wins and one draw putting them on 40 points.

In the last 10 years, Keith and Nairn have played each other on 13 occasions. Keith won one of them, Nairn seven, and they drew five times.

On average, Keith scored 1.2 goals and Nairn 2.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 4, when Nairn won 3-2 at home.

With 23 goals in 19 games so far this season, Keith are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.2 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 44 goals at a rate of 2.3 per game.

County, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 2.0 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.9. They have conceded 1.6 goals per game.

Keith's last match was on February 3, a 2-0 loss against Banks O' Dee.

Nairn beat Buckie Thistle 3-0 last time out, also on February 3.
