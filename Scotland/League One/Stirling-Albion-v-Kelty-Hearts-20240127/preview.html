Stirling Albion v Kelty Hearts

League One | Forthbank Stadium
Saturday, 27 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Struggling Stirling Albion face Kelty Hearts at the Forthbank Stadium on Saturday looking to build on a win in their last league outing.

After securing all three points with a 1-0 victory over Montrose on January 13, the Binos sit eighth in League One.

They travel to play a Kelty Hearts side sixth in the standings, who were held in their last match, 1-1 against Annan Athletic.

In the last 10 years, Stirling and Kelty Hearts have played each other on six occasions. Stirling won one of them, Kelty Hearts three, and they drew twice.

On average, the Binos scored 0.7 goals and Kelty Hearts 1.5 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 28, when Kelty Hearts won 1-0 at home.

In Blair Currie, Stirling can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept five clean sheets in his 19 appearances this season, and only two other 'keepers – Falkirk's Sam Long and Hamilton Academical's Ryan Fulton – have been able to prevent the opposition scoring on more occasions in League One.

In Kelty Hearts's net, Kyle Gourlay has two clean sheets in 19 games.

With 16 goals in 19 games so far this season, Albion are the league's lowest scorers with 0.8 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 32 goals at a rate of 1.7 per game.

The visitors are also below average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

The Binos are in mixed form in League One, with two wins and two draws from their last six games.

With a win and three draws over that period, Kelty Hearts's form is slightly worse – they have taken six points from 18, compared to the home side's eight.
