Buxton v Scunthorpe United

National League North and South | The Silverlands
Saturday, 23 December 2023 @ 3.00pm

National League North and South's top two sides face each other at the Silverlands in Saturday's kick-off, when zero-placed Buxton host second-placed Scunthorpe United.

Buxton have picked up eight wins and five draws from 22 games so far this season, and sit 17 points below the visitors going into the 3pm match.

The Iron, meanwhile, have won 14 and drawn four, picking up 46 points.

With 49 goals in 22 games so far this season, Scunthorpe are the league's highest scorers with 2.2 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 19 goals at a rate of 0.9 per game.

Buxton are also above average scorers, with 1.6 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.4. They have conceded 1.2 goals per game.

In Danny Whitehall, United have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 13 goals in 22 appearances, to sit second in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 127 minutes is slightly quicker than that of Diego Raymond De Girolamo, the hosts' top scorer with a goal every 127 minutes, and a total of nine goals in 18 games.

Buxton are in disappointing form in National League North and South, with two wins and four losses from their last six games.

With five wins and one loss over that period, the Iron's form is much better – they have taken 15 points from 18, compared to Buxton's six.

Buxton's last match was on December 16, a 3-2 win against Spennymoor Town, with Connor Alexander Kirby, De Girolamo and Jake Wright Jr. getting the goals for Buxton.

Scunthorpe beat Chorley 3-0 last time out, also on December 16, with Whitehall (two) and Connor Smith on the scoresheet.
