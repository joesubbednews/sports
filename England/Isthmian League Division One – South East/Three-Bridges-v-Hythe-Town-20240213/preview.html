Three Bridges v Hythe Town

Isthmian League Division One – South East | Jubilee Field
Tuesday, 13 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Three Bridges face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form at home against a tight Hythe Town defence on Tuesday.

With 58 goals in 24 games, Three Bridges are the third-highest scorers in the Isthmian League Division One South East ahead of the 7.45pm kick-off at Jubilee Field.

They face a Cannons side who have scored 35 in 19 matches, but conceded only 27 goals, putting them fourth among the league's tightest defences – only Ramsgate, Cray Valley PM and Sittingbourne have conceded fewer goals.

Three Bridges are in bad form in the Isthmian League Division One South East, with one win and five losses from their last six games.

With no wins and four draws over that period, Hythe's form is slightly better – they have taken four points from 18, compared to the home side's three.

In the last five years, Three Bridges and Hythe have played each other on four occasions. Hythe won one of them and they drew the other.

On average, Three Bridges scored 1.8 goals and the Cannons 2.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on February 25, when they played out a 1-1 draw.

Three Bridges are eighth in the table after 24 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn four, earning 37 points.

Town are four places behind Three Bridges in 12th, with eight wins and five draws putting them on 29 points.

Three Bridges's last match was on February 6, a 2-0 loss against Cray Valley PM.

Hythe drew 4-4 with Littlehampton Town last time out, on Saturday.
