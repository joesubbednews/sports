Lancing v Cray Valley PM

Isthmian League Division One – South East | Culver Road
Saturday, 20 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Two of Isthmian League Division One South East's top sides face each other at Culver Road in Saturday's kick-off, when third-placed Lancing host second-placed Cray Valley PM.

Lancing have picked up 13 wins and two draws from 21 games so far this season, and sit on the same number of points as the visitors going into the 3pm match.

Cray Valley PM, meanwhile, have won 12 and drawn five of 17, picking up 41 points.

With 46 goals in 17 games so far this season, Cray Valley PM are the league's third-highest scorers with 2.7 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 16 goals at a rate of 0.9 per game.

Lancing are also above average scorers, with 2.0 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.8. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

The home side are in fantastic form in Isthmian League Division One South East, with five wins and a draw from their last six games.

With four wins and two draws over that period, the visitors' form is slightly worse – they have taken 14 points from 18, compared to Lancing's 16.

In the last 10 years, Lancing and Cray Valley PM have played each other on six occasions. Lancing won one of them, Cray Valley PM three, and they drew twice.

On average, Lancing scored 1.3 goals and Cray Valley PM 2.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on January 9, when they played out a 1-1 draw.

Lancing's last match was on Tuesday, a 2-1 win against Hythe Town.

Cray Valley PM beat Sheppey United 3-1 last time out, on January 13.
