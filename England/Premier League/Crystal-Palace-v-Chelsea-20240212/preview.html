Crystal Palace v Chelsea

Premier League | Selhurst Park
Monday, 12 February 2024 @ 8.00pm

Chelsea are strong favourites to take all three points despite Crystal Palace's home advantage in Monday's late match at Selhurst Park.

*Betting Company* are offering odds of 1.77 on Chelsea sealing the win, with the visitors sitting 11th in the Premier League table.

Palace, who are 16th in the league and seven points behind the Blues, are priced at 4.4 to win. A draw is set at 3.85.

In the last 10 years, Palace and Chelsea have played each other on 21 occasions. Palace won four of them and Chelsea 17.

On average, the Eagles scored 0.7 goals and the Blues 1.9 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on December 27, when Chelsea won 2-1 at home.

With 26 goals in 23 games so far this season, Palace are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.1 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 40 goals at a rate of 1.7 per game.

Chelsea, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.7 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have also conceded 1.7 goals per game.

The Eagles are in mixed form in the Premier League, with two wins and a draw from their last six games.

With three wins and three losses over that period, the Blues' form is slightly better – they have taken nine points from 18, compared to the hosts' seven.

Palace's last match was on February 3, a 4-1 loss against Brighton and Hove Albion, with Jean-Philippe Mateta getting the goal for the Eagles.

Chelsea lost 4-2 against Wolverhampton Wanderers last time out, on February 4, with Cole Palmer and Thiago Silva on the scoresheet.

Monday's match will be refereed by Michael Oliver, who has taken charge of 14 Premier League games so far this season, issuing four red cards and booking 57 players. He has awarded four penalties.

The last Palace game Oliver refereed was a 1-1 away draw with West Ham United on December 3. His last Chelsea match was their 2-0 loss away at Everton on December 10.
