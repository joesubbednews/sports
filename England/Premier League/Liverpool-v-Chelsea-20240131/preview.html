Liverpool v Chelsea

Premier League | Anfield
Wednesday, 31 January 2024 @ 8.15pm

Liverpool are heavy favourites to keep all three points at home in Wednesday's late kick-off against Chelsea.

The Reds, who sit top of the Premier League with 21 games played, are priced at 1.6 to seal victory at Anfield.

Sitting eight places and 17 points behind them in the table, Chelsea are 5.1 to win with *Betting Company*, while the draw is at 4.0.

With 47 goals in 21 games so far this season, Liverpool are the league's second-highest scorers with 2.2 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 18 goals at a rate of 0.9 per game.

Chelsea are also above average scorers, with 1.7 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

In Mohamed Salah, the Reds have the league's sharpest shooter so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 20 appearances.

His goal rate of one every 125 minutes is quicker than that of Nicolas Jackson, the Blues' top scorer with a goal every 208 minutes, and a total of seven goals in 19 games.

In the last 10 years, Liverpool and Chelsea have played each other on 28 occasions. Liverpool won six of them, Chelsea eight, and they drew 14 times.

On average, the Reds scored 1.1 goals and the Blues 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 13, when they played out a 1-1 draw.

The hosts are in good form in the Premier League, with four wins and two draws from their last six games.

With four wins and two losses over that period, the away side's form is slightly worse – they have taken 12 points from 18, compared to Liverpool's 14.

Liverpool's last match was on January 21, a 4-0 win against AFC Bournemouth, with Darwin Núñez (two) and Diogo Jota (two) getting the goals for the Reds.

Chelsea beat Fulham 1-0 last time out, on January 13, with Cole Palmer on the scoresheet.

Wednesday's match will be refereed by Paul Tierney, who has taken charge of 13 Premier League games so far this season, issuing three red cards and booking 45 players. He has awarded three penalties.

The last Liverpool game Tierney refereed was a 2-0 away win against Burnley on December 26. His last Chelsea match was their 3-2 win away at Luton Town on December 30.
