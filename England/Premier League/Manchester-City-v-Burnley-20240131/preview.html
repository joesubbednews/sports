Manchester City v Burnley

Premier League | Etihad Stadium
Wednesday, 31 January 2024 @ 7.30pm

Mid-season relegation candidates Burnley face a challenge away against high-flying Manchester City at the Etihad Stadium on Wednesday.

Burnley are 19th in the Premier League table, and have picked up three wins and three draws in their 21 games to date.

The Citizens, meanwhile, are second in the standings with 43 points, having won 13 and drawn four of their first 20 matches, and are five points behind table-toppers Liverpool.

With 48 goals in 20 games so far this season, City are the league's highest scorers with 2.4 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 23 goals at a rate of 1.1 per game.

Burnley, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.0 goal per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 2.0 goals per game.

In Erling Haaland, the Citizens have the league's most on-form striker so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 15 appearances.

The Clarets's top scorers, with four goals each, are Lyle Foster and Zeki Amdouni.

In the last 10 years, City and Burnley have played each other on 19 occasions. City won 16 of them, Burnley one, and they drew twice.

On average, the Citizens scored 3.0 goals and the Clarets 0.4 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 11, when City won 3-0 away.

The hosts are in good form in the Premier League, with four wins and a draw from their last six games.

With a win and two draws over that period, the away side's form is much worse – they have taken five points from 18, compared to City's 13.

City's last match was on January 13, a 3-2 win against Newcastle United, with Bernardo Silva, Kevin De Bruyne and Oscar Bobb getting the goals for the Citizens.

Burnley drew 1-1 with Luton Town last time out, on January 12, with Amdouni on the scoresheet.
