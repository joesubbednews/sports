Manchester City v Brentford

Premier League | Etihad Stadium
Friday, 22 December 2023 @ 11.00pm

Brentford make the journey to the Etihad Stadium to play Manchester City on late Friday looking to pick up points to end their three-game losing streak.

Brentford's struggles have left them with just three points from their last six Premier League matches, while their opponents have earned seven from a possible 18.

With 40 goals in 17 games so far this season, City are the league's highest scorers with 2.4 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 20 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

Brentford, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have also conceded 1.4 goals per game.

In Erling Haaland, the Citizens have the league's most on-form striker so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 15 appearances.

His goal rate of one every 93 minutes is quicker than that of Bryan Mbeumo, the Bees' top scorer with a goal every 184 minutes, and a total of seven goals in 15 games.

The hosts are fourth in the table after 17 games, of which they have won 10 and drawn four, earning 34 points.

The away side are nine places behind City in 13th, with five wins and four draws putting them on 19 points.

City's last match was on Saturday, a 2-2 draw against Crystal Palace, with Jack Grealish and Rico Lewis getting the goals for the Citizens.

Brentford lost 2-1 against Aston Villa last time out, on Sunday, with Keane Lewis-Potter on the scoresheet.
