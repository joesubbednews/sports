Liverpool v Burnley

Premier League | Anfield
Saturday, 10 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Liverpool host Burnley in Saturday's match at Anfield looking to bounce back from defeat last time out in the Premier League.

The Reds, who sit top of the league after 23 games, fell to a 3-1 away defeat to Arsenal on February 4.

They face a Burnley side who secured a draw in their last match, a 2-2 tie with Fulham, and who sit 19th in the table.

With 52 goals in 23 games so far this season, Liverpool are the league's highest scorers with 2.3 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 22 goals at a rate of 1.0 per game.

Burnley, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.0 goal per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 2.0 goals per game.

In Mohamed Salah, the Reds have the league's sharpest shooter so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 20 appearances.

The Clarets's top scorers, with four goals each, are Lyle Foster and Zeki Amdouni.

In the last 10 years, Liverpool and Burnley have played each other on 15 occasions. Liverpool won 11 of them, Burnley two, and they drew twice.

On average, the Reds scored 1.8 goals and the Clarets 0.7 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on December 26, when Liverpool won 2-0 away.

The home side are in good form in the Premier League, with four wins and a draw from their last six games.

With a win and two draws over that period, the visitors' form is much worse – they have taken five points from 18, compared to Liverpool's 13.
