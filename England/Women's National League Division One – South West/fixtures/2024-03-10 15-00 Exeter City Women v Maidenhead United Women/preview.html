Exeter City Women v Maidenhead United Women

Women's National League Division One – South West
Sunday, 10 March 2024 @ 2.00pm

Exeter City Women face Maidenhead United Women on Sunday seeking to protect their formidable unbeaten run in the Women's National League Division One South West.

Exeter City are unbeaten in 15, with 12 wins and three draws, ahead of the 2pm kick-off.

They face a Maidenhead United team who have won three and drawn five over the same number of games.

With 72 goals in 15 games so far this season, Exeter City are the league's highest scorers with 4.8 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 13 goals at a rate of 0.9 per game.

Maidenhead United, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.7 goals per game, compared to a league average of 2.4. They have conceded 1.9 goals per game.

The hosts are top of the table after 15 games, of which they have won 12 and drawn three, earning 39 points.

The away side are seven places behind Exeter City in eighth, with three wins and five draws putting them on 14 points.

In the last five years, Exeter City and Maidenhead United have played each other on five occasions. Exeter City won four of them and Maidenhead United one.

On average, Exeter City scored 3.2 goals and Maidenhead United 0.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 8, when Exeter City won 6-2 away.

Exeter City's last match was on February 22, a 0-0 draw against Torquay United Women.

Maidenhead United drew 0-0 with Southampton WFC last time out, on March 3.
