Maidenhead United Women v AFC Bournemouth Women

Women's National League Division One – South West | York Road Stadium
Sunday, 17 March 2024 @ 2.00pm

Maidenhead United Women and AFC Bournemouth Women come into Sunday's early kick-off at the York Road Stadium in contrasting spells, with the away team hitting a rich vein of form while the hosts struggle.

Maidenhead United have drawn three and lost three of the last six, while Bournemouth Women have taken five wins and one draw.

With 57 goals in 13 games so far this season, Bournemouth Women are the league's third-highest scorers with 4.4 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in seven goals at a rate of 0.5 per game.

Maidenhead United, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.5 goals per game, compared to a league average of 2.3. They have conceded 2.0 goals per game.

The away side are second in the table after 13 games, of which they have won 12 and drawn one, earning 37 points.

The hosts are six places behind Bournemouth Women in eighth, with three wins and five draws putting them on 14 points.

In the last five years, Maidenhead United and Bournemouth Women have played each other on five occasions. Bournemouth Women won three of them and they drew twice.

On average, Maidenhead United scored 0.8 goals and Bournemouth Women 2.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 17, when Bournemouth Women won 4-1 at home.

Maidenhead United's last match was on Wednesday, a 1-0 loss against Swindon Town.

Bournemouth Women beat Abingdon United Women 6-0 last time out, on March 10.
