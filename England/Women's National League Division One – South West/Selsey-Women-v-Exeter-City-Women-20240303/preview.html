Selsey Women v Exeter City Women

Women's National League Division One – South West
Sunday, 3 March 2024 @ 2.00pm

Mid-season relegation candidates Selsey Women face a challenge against high-flying Exeter City Women on Sunday.

Selsey Women are rooted to the bottom of the Women's National League Division One – South West table, and have picked up no wins and no draws in their 14 games to date.

Exeter City, meanwhile, are top of the standings with 39 points, having won 12 and drawn three of their first 15 matches.

Selsey are in awful form in the Women's National League Division One South West, with no wins and six losses from their last six games.

With three wins and three draws over that period, Exeter City's form is much better – they have taken 12 points from 18, compared to the hosts' zero.

With two goals in 14 games so far this season, Selsey are the league's lowest scorers with 0.1 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 115 goals at a rate of 8.2 per game.

The away team, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 4.8 goals per game, compared to a league average of 2.5. They have conceded 0.9 goals per game.

In the last three years, Selsey and Exeter City have played each other on three occasions. Exeter City won all of them.

Their last meeting was on January 21, when Exeter City won 14-0 at home.

Selsey's last match was on February 11, a 5-0 loss against Keynsham Town LFC.

Exeter City drew 0-0 with Torquay United Women last time out, on February 22.
