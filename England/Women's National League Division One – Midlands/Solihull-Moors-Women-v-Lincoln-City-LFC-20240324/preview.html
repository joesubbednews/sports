Solihull Moors Women v Lincoln City LFC

Women's National League Division One – Midlands
Sunday, 24 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Solihull Moors Women face Lincoln City LFC on Sunday looking to secure a first win in nine Women's National League Division One – Midlands games.

Solihull Moors have lost four and drawn four matches since they last earned three points – against Leafield Athletic Women on November 16.

They face a Lincoln City side who have won two and drawn four over that time.

In the last 10 years, Solihull Moors and Lincoln City have played each other on six occasions. They won three each.

On average, Solihull Moors scored 2.0 goals and Lincoln City 2.0 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 8, when Solihull Moors won 3-2 away.

With 17 goals in 17 games so far this season, Solihull Moors are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.0 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 27 goals at a rate of 1.6 per game.

Lincoln City, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 2.0 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.8. They have also conceded 2.0 goals per game.

The visitors are eighth in the table after 17 games, of which they have won four and drawn seven, earning 19 points.

The home team are one place ahead of Lincoln City in seventh, with five wins and five draws putting them on 20 points.

Solihull Moors's last match was on Tuesday, a 6-0 loss against Northampton Town Women.

Lincoln City drew 1-1 with Northampton Town Women last time out, on March 17.
