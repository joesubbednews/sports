Sheffield FC Women v Peterborough United Women

Women's National League Division One – Midlands
Sunday, 10 March 2024 @ 4.30pm

Mid-season relegation candidates Sheffield FC Women face a challenge against high-flying Peterborough United Women on Sunday.

Sheffield FC Women are 11th in the Women's National League Division One – Midlands table, and have picked up just one win and four draws in their 13 games to date.

Peterborough United, meanwhile, are fourth in the standings with 28 points, having won nine and drawn one of their first 14 matches, and are seven points behind table-toppers Loughborough Lightning Women.

With six goals in 13 games so far this season, Sheffield FC are the league's lowest scorers with 0.5 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 21 goals at a rate of 1.6 per game.

Peterborough United, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 2.6 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.8. They have conceded 1.6 goals per game.

The hosts are in bad form in the Women's National League Division One Midlands, with one win and a draw from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, the away team's form is much better – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to Sheffield FC's four.

In the last three years, Sheffield FC and Peterborough United have played each other on three occasions. They won one each, and they drew once.

Their last meeting was on September 24, when Peterborough United won 4-0 at home.

Sheffield FC's last match was on February 4, a 1-0 loss against Boldmere St. Michaels Women.

Peterborough United drew 2-2 with Sporting Khalsa Women last time out, on February 25.
