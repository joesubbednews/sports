Millwall v Sheffield Wednesday

EFL Championship | The Den
Saturday, 17 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Saturday's match at the Den sees two relegation candidates play each other, as 21st-ranked Millwall host 23rd-placed Sheffield Wednesday.

Millwall have picked up 33 points from their first 33 EFL Championship games, with eight wins and nine draws.

That is seven points more than the Owls have collected, having won seven and drawn five.

Millwall are in terrible form in EFL Championship, with no wins and a draw from their last six games.

With a win and a draw over that period, Wednesday's form is better – they have taken four points from 18, compared to the Lions's one.

In the last 10 years, Millwall and Wednesday have played each other on 11 occasions. Millwall won four of them, Wednesday three, and they drew four times.

On average, the Lions scored 1.4 goals and the Owls 0.9 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 11, when Millwall won 4-0 away.

With 24 goals in 32 games so far this season, the Owls are the league's lowest scorers with 0.8 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 52 goals at a rate of 1.6 per game.

The home team are also below average scorers, with 1.0 goal per game, compared to a league average of 1.4. They have conceded 1.4 goals per game.

Millwall's last match was on Wednesday, a 4-0 loss against Ipswich Town.

Wednesday lost 2-0 against Leicester City last time out, on Tuesday.
