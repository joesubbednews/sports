Blackburn Rovers v Stoke City

EFL Championship | Ewood Park
Saturday, 10 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Blackburn Rovers are on a terrible run ahead of hosting Stoke City at Ewood Park on Saturday, with just two points collected from their last six games.

Blackburn have picked up two draws and four losses in their last six EFL Championship games, and face a Potters side whose last six games have brought one win and two draws.

In Samuel Szmodics, Blackburn have the league's sharpest shooter so far this season. He has notched 16 goals in 27 appearances.

His goal rate of one every 149 minutes is quicker than that of André Vidigal, Stoke's top scorer with a goal every 284 minutes, and a total of five goals in 22 games.

In the last 10 years, Blackburn and Stoke have played each other on 12 occasions. They won five each, and they drew twice.

On average, Blackburn scored 1.3 goals and the Potters 1.0 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 25, when Blackburn won 3-0 away.

City are 20th in the table after 30 games, of which they have won eight and drawn eight, earning 32 points.

Rovers are two places ahead of the Potters in 18th, with 10 wins and three draws putting them on 33 points.

With 28 goals in 30 games so far this season, the away team are scoring at below the league average rate with 0.9 goals per game. And they are conceding at an average rate, letting in 42 goals at a rate of 1.4 per game.

The hosts, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game. They have conceded 1.9 goals per game.

Blackburn's last match was on February 3, a 2-1 loss against Queens Park Rangers, with Sam Gallagher getting the goal for Blackburn.

Stoke lost 5-0 against Leicester City last time out, also on February 3.
