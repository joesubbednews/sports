Huddersfield Town v Sunderland

EFL Championship | John Smith's Stadium
Wednesday, 14 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Sunderland are strong favourites to take all three points despite Huddersfield Town's home advantage in Wednesday's late match at the John Smith's Stadium.

*Betting Company* are offering odds of 1.92 on Sunderland sealing the win, with the visitors sitting seventh in EFL Championship table.

Huddersfield, who are 21st in the league and 16 points behind the Black Cats, are priced at 3.87 to win. A draw is set at 3.55.

With 36 goals in 31 games so far this season, Huddersfield are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.2 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 52 goals at a rate of 1.7 per game.

Sunderland, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game. They have conceded 1.1 goals per game.

In Jack Clarke, the Black Cats have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 31 appearances, to sit fourth in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 197 minutes is quicker than that of Michal Helik, Town's top scorer with a goal every 324 minutes, and a total of eight goals in 29 games.

The Terriers are in disappointing form in EFL Championship, with one win and three draws from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, the away side's form is better – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to the hosts' six.

In the last three years, Huddersfield and Sunderland have played each other on three occasions. They won one each, and they drew once.

Their last meeting was on November 29, when Huddersfield won 2-1 away.

Huddersfield's last match was on Saturday, a 5-3 loss against Southampton, with Alex Matos, David Kasumu and Sorba Thomas getting the goals for the Terriers.

Sunderland beat Plymouth Argyle 3-1 last time out, also on Saturday, with Clarke, Jobe Bellingham and Pierre Ekwah on the scoresheet.

Wednesday's match will be refereed by Gavin Ward, who has taken charge of 16 EFL Championship games so far this season, issuing two red cards and booking 63 players. He has awarded two penalties.

The last Huddersfield game Ward refereed was a 2-1 home win against Queens Park Rangers on October 21. His last Sunderland match was their 2-1 win at home against Rotherham United on August 19.
