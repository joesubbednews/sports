Barnet v Kidderminster Harriers

National League | The Hive Stadium
Saturday, 20 April 2024 @ 12.15pm

Relegation candidates Kidderminster Harriers face a challenge away against high-flying Barnet at the Hive Stadium on Saturday.

Kidderminster Harriers are 22nd in the National League table, and have picked up 11 wins and 13 draws in their 45 games to date.

The Bees, meanwhile, are second in the standings with 83 points, having won 25 and drawn eight, and are 12 points behind table-toppers Chesterfield.

With 39 goals in 45 games so far this season, Kidderminster Harriers are the league's lowest scorers with 0.9 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 55 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

Barnet, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.3 goals per game.

The Bees are in reasonable form in the National League, with three wins and three draws from their last six games.

With a win and two draws over that period, Kiddy's form is much worse – they have taken five points from 18, compared to the home side's 12.

Barnet's last match was on Saturday, a 2-0 win against Halifax Town, with Callum Stead and Daniele James Collinge getting the goals for the Bees.

Kidderminster Harriers lost 1-0 against Eastleigh last time out, also on Saturday.
