Halifax Town v Maidenhead United

National League | The Shay Stadium
Tuesday, 13 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Halifax Town are on a poor run ahead of hosting Maidenhead United at the Shay Stadium on Tuesday, with just four points collected from their last six games.

The Shaymen have picked up one win and one draw in their last six National League games, and face a Maidenhead side whose last six games have brought two wins and two draws.

In the last 10 years, Halifax and Maidenhead have played each other on 16 occasions. Halifax won five of them, Maidenhead eight, and they drew three times.

On average, the Shaymen scored 1.4 goals and Maidenhead 1.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 11, when Maidenhead won 1-0 at home.

Halifax are 10th in the table after 32 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn 11, earning 44 points.

Maidenhead are five places behind Town in 15th, with nine wins and 12 draws putting them on 39 points.

With 32 goals in 32 games so far this season, United are the league's joint-third-lowest scorers with 1.0 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 39 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

The Shaymen are also below average scorers, with 1.2 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have also conceded 1.2 goals per game.

Halifax's last match was on Saturday, a 3-1 win against Dorking Wanderers, with Max Wright (two) and Andrew Oluwabori getting the goals for the Shaymen.

Maidenhead lost 1-0 against Boreham Wood last time out, also on Saturday.
