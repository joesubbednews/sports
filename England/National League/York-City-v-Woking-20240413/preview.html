York City v Woking

National League | LNER Community Stadium
Saturday, 13 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

York City host Woking on Saturday at the LNER Community Stadium in the National League.

In their last league match, on April 6, York lost to Eastleigh 1-0 at home.

Woking won, 3-2 at home against Dagenham and Redbridge, with Jayden Luker, Jermaine Barrington Anderson and Kevin Berkoe on the scoresheet.

York are 19th in the table after 44 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn 17, earning 50 points.

Woking are three places ahead of City in 16th, with 14 wins and 10 draws putting them on 52 points.

With 46 goals in 44 games so far this season, the Cardinals are the league's second-lowest scorers with 1.0 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 53 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

The hosts are also below average scorers, with 1.2 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

In the last three years, York and Woking have played each other on three occasions. York won all of them.

Their last meeting was on September 23, when York won 2-1 away.

York are in mixed form in the National League, with three wins and a draw from their last six games.

With two wins and two draws over that period, the away side's form is slightly worse – they have taken eight points from 18, compared to York's 10.
