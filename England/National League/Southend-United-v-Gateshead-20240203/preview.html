Southend United v Gateshead

National League | Roots Hall
Saturday, 3 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Gateshead face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form away against a tight Southend United defence on Saturday.

With 52 goals in 28 games, Gateshead are the joint-fourth-highest scorers in National League ahead of the 3pm kick-off at Roots Hall.

They face a Shrimpers side who have scored 44 in 28 matches, but conceded only 31 goals, putting them joint-top of the league's tightest defences.

Southend are seventh in the table after 28 games, of which they have won 13 and drawn five, earning 44 points.

Gateshead are two places behind United in ninth, with 12 wins and seven draws putting them on 43 points.

The Shrimpers are in mixed form in National League, with three wins and three losses from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, the Tynesiders's form is slightly worse – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to the home side's nine.

In the last three years, Southend and Gateshead have played each other on three occasions. They won one each, and they drew once.

Their last meeting was on November 25, when they played out a 1-1 draw.

Southend's last match was on January 27, a 3-0 loss against Chesterfield.

Gateshead beat Barnet 2-1 last time out, also on January 27, with Kain Adom and Luke Hannant on the scoresheet.
