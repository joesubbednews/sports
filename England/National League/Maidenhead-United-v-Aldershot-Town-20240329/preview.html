Maidenhead United v Aldershot Town

National League | York Road Stadium
Friday, 29 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Maidenhead United host Aldershot Town on Friday at the York Road Stadium in the National League.

In their last league match, on Saturday, Maidenhead lost to Rochdale 1-0 away.

Aldershot drew, 1-1 at home against Solihull Moors, with Cian Harries scoring their goals.

With 44 goals in 40 games so far this season, Maidenhead are the league's third-lowest scorers with 1.1 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 58 goals at a rate of 1.4 per game.

Aldershot, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.7 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.8 goals per game.

In the last 10 years, Maidenhead and Aldershot have played each other on 13 occasions. Maidenhead won two of them, Aldershot five, and they drew six times.

On average, Maidenhead scored 1.4 goals and the Shots 1.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 24, when Aldershot won 4-2 at home.

United are 20th in the table after 40 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn 12, earning 45 points.

Town are 15 places ahead of the home side in fifth, with 19 wins and seven draws putting them on 64 points.

Maidenhead are in disappointing form in the National League, with two wins and four losses from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, the Shots' form is better – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to Maidenhead's six.
