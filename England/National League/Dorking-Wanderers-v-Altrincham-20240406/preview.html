Dorking Wanderers v Altrincham

National League | Meadowbank Stadium
Saturday, 6 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Relegation candidates Dorking Wanderers face a challenge against high-flying Altrincham at the Meadowbank Stadium on Saturday.

Dorking Wanderers are 23rd in the National League table, and have picked up 12 wins and seven draws in their 42 games to date.

Robins, meanwhile, are fourth in the standings with 70 points, having won 20 and drawn 10 of their first 43 matches, and are 25 points behind table-toppers Chesterfield.

With 81 goals in 43 games so far this season, Altrincham are scoring more than average in the league with 1.9 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 59 goals at a rate of 1.4 per game.

Dorking, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.8 goals per game.

Wanderers are in terrible form in the National League, with no wins and two draws from their last six games.

With five wins and one loss over that period, Robins' form is much better – they have taken 15 points from 18, compared to the home team's two.

In the last three years, Dorking and Altrincham have played each other on three occasions. Dorking won one of them and Altrincham the other.

Their last meeting was on March 5, when Altrincham won 2-1 at home.

Dorking's last match was on Monday, a 1-1 draw against Aldershot Town, with Alfie Rutherford getting the goal for Wanderers.

Altrincham beat Oldham Athletic 1-0 last time out, also on Monday, with Christopher Conn on the scoresheet.
