Bromley v Solihull Moors

National League | Hayes Lane
Saturday, 13 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Two of the National League's top sides face each other at Hayes Lane in Saturday's kick-off, when third-placed Bromley host fourth-placed Solihull Moors.

Bromley have picked up 20 wins and 15 draws from 43 games so far this season, and sit two points above the visitors going into the 3pm match.

The Moors, meanwhile, have won 20 and drawn 13 of 44, picking up 73 points.

With 70 goals in 44 games so far this season, Solihull Moors are scoring more than average in the league with 1.6 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 59 goals at a rate of 1.3 per game.

Bromley, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.5 goals per game. They have conceded 1.1 goals per game.

In the last 10 years, Bromley and Solihull Moors have played each other on 17 occasions. Bromley won six of them, Solihull Moors eight, and they drew three times.

On average, the Ravens scored 1.1 goals and the Moors 1.5 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on April 6, when Solihull Moors won 2-1 away.

The Ravens are in mixed form in the National League, with two wins and three draws from their last six games.

And also with two wins and three draws over that period, the Moors's form is identical – they have both taken nine points from 18.

Bromley's last match was on Tuesday, a 1-1 draw against Barnet, with Louis Hugh Dennis getting the goal for the Ravens.

Solihull Moors beat Ebbsfleet United 3-0 last time out, also on Tuesday, with Tyrese Shade (two) and Jamey Osborne on the scoresheet.
