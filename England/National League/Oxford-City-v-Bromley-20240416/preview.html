Oxford City v Bromley

National League | RAW Charging Stadium
Tuesday, 16 April 2024 @ 7.45pm

Relegation candidates Oxford City face a challenge against high-flying Bromley at the RAW Charging Stadium on Tuesday.

Oxford City are rooted to the bottom of the National League table, and have picked up eight wins and nine draws in their 44 games to date.

The Ravens, meanwhile, are third in the standings with 78 points, having won 21 and drawn 15, and are 17 points behind table-toppers Chesterfield.

With 53 goals in 44 games so far this season, Oxford are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.2 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 90 goals at a rate of 2.0 per game.

Bromley, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.6 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.0 goal per game.

City are in bad form in the National League, with one win and a draw from their last six games.

With three wins and two draws over that period, the Ravens's form is much better – they have taken 11 points from 18, compared to the home team's four.

Oxford's last match was on Saturday, a 0-0 draw against Oldham Athletic.

Bromley beat Solihull Moors 3-0 last time out, also on Saturday, with Ben Krauhaus, Jude Arthurs and Michael Cheek on the scoresheet.
