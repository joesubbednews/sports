Gateshead v Eastleigh

National League | Gateshead International Stadium
Tuesday, 6 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Gateshead are heavy favourites to keep all three points at home in Tuesday's late kick-off against Eastleigh.

The Tynesiders, who sit seventh in National League with 29 games played, are priced at 1.4 to seal victory at the Gateshead International Stadium.

Sitting seven places and eight points behind them in the table, Eastleigh are 6.5 to win with *Betting Company*, while the draw is at 4.9.

Gateshead are in mixed form in National League, with two wins and a draw from their last six games.

With no wins and two draws over that period, Eastleigh's form is worse – they have taken two points from 18, compared to the Tynesiders's seven.

With 53 goals in 29 games so far this season, the home team are scoring more than average in the league with 1.8 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 40 goals at a rate of 1.4 per game.

The Spitfires are also above average scorers, with 1.7 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 2.0 goals per game.

In the last 10 years, Gateshead and Eastleigh have played each other on 13 occasions. Gateshead won five of them, Eastleigh four, and they drew four times.

On average, the Tynesiders scored 2.1 goals and the Spitfires 1.4 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 9, when Gateshead won 6-0 away.

Gateshead's last match was on Saturday, a 1-0 win against Southend United, with Marcus Dinanga Nyamabu getting the goal for the Tynesiders.

Eastleigh lost 3-1 against Chesterfield last time out, also on Saturday, with Chris Maguire on the scoresheet.
