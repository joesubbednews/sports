Fylde v Wealdstone

National League | Mill Farm Stadium
Saturday, 16 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Wealdstone travel to Fylde on Saturday in the National League.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a draw in their last match, having tied with Southend United 1-1 away, with a goal from Sean Jordon Adarkwa.

The Coasters also drew their last match, 1-1 against Kidderminster Harriers, with their goal scored by Nicholas George Haughton.

With 65 goals in 39 games so far this season, Fylde are scoring more than average in the league with 1.7 goals per game. But they are conceding more than average too, letting in 71 goals at a rate of 1.8 per game.

Wealdstone, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

The visitors are 20th in the table after 34 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn 10, earning 43 points.

The Coasters are six places ahead of Wealdstone in 14th, with 12 wins and 10 draws putting them on 46 points.

The home team are in mixed form in the National League, with two wins and two draws from their last six games.

With a win and two draws over that period, Wealdstone's form is worse – they have taken five points from 18, compared to Fylde's eight.
