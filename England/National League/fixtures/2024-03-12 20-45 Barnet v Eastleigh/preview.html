Barnet v Eastleigh

National League | The Hive Stadium
Tuesday, 12 March 2024 @ 7.45pm

Tuesday's late match between Barnet and Eastleigh promises to be one for the neutrals, as two of the National League's most free-scoring sides go head-to-head.

Ahead of the game at the Hive Stadium, the Bees and the Spitfires sit joint-second and joint-fourth in the goal-scoring charts, with 69 and 64 goals respectively.

Striker Nicke Kabamba leads the way for the home side, having bagged 18 goals in their 37 games to date.

And Paul Leon Miller McCallum has been the main man in the opponents' penalty box for Eastleigh, with 29 goals.

Barnet are second in the table after 37 games, of which they have won 21 and drawn five, earning 68 points.

Eastleigh are 10 places behind the Bees in 12th, with 12 wins and 10 draws putting them on 46 points.

In the last 10 years, Barnet and Eastleigh have played each other on 12 occasions. Barnet won seven of them, Eastleigh three, and they drew twice.

On average, the Bees scored 1.8 goals and the Spitfires 1.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 15, when they played out a 3-3 draw.
