Bromley v Woking

National League | Hayes Lane
Monday, 1 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Two of the National League's meanest defences go head-to-head at Hayes Lane on Monday, when Bromley host Woking.

Only one side – Southend United – has conceded fewer goals than Bromley to date: the home side have let in just 44 goals in 41 games.

The Cardinals have conceded 50 goals in 42 games, giving them the fourth tightest back line so far this season.

Key to the Ravens's home form has been Grant Ashley Smith, who has allowed 0.99 goals past him per 90 minutes, compared to 1 for William Jääskeläinen in the opposite net.

Bromley are third in the table after 41 games, of which they have won 20 and drawn 13, earning 73 points.

Woking are 14 places behind the Ravens in 17th, with 13 wins and nine draws putting them on 48 points.

In the last 10 years, Bromley and Woking have played each other on 17 occasions. Bromley won 10 of them, Woking five, and they drew twice.

On average, the Ravens scored 1.5 goals and the Cardinals 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 28, when Bromley won 1-0 away.

The home side are in reasonable form in the National League, with three wins and two draws from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, the Cardinals's form is slightly worse – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to Bromley's 11.

Bromley's last match was on Friday, a 2-0 win against Dorking Wanderers, with Callum Reynolds and Michael Cheek getting the goals for the Ravens.

Woking lost 1-0 against Eastleigh last time out, also on Friday.
