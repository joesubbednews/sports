Manchester City Women v Leicester City WFC

FA Women's Super League | Academy Stadium
Sunday, 4 February 2024 @ 1.00pm

Manchester City Women are heavy favourites to keep all three points at home in Sunday lunchtime's kick-off against Leicester City WFC.

City, who sit second in FA Women's Super League with 12 games played, are priced at 1.1 to seal victory at the Academy Stadium.

Sitting five places and 15 points behind them in the table, Leicester City WFC are 18.0 to win with *Betting Company*, while the draw is at 9.0.

Manchester City are in exceptional form in FA Women's Super League, with six wins and no losses from their last six games.

With a win and three draws over that period, Leicester City WFC's form is much worse – they have taken six points from 18, compared to City's 18.

With 33 goals in 12 games so far this season, the home team are the league's second-highest scorers with 2.8 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in eight goals at a rate of 0.7 per game.

The visitors, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.3 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 1.8 goals per game.

In Khadija Monifa Shaw, Manchester City have the league's most on-form striker so far this season. She has notched 13 goals in 11 appearances.

Leicester City WFC's top scorers, with four goals in 12 games each, are Jutta Rantala and Lena Petermann.

In the last 10 years, Manchester City and Leicester City WFC have played each other on 12 occasions. Manchester City won 11 of them and they drew once.

On average, City scored 3.3 goals and Leicester City WFC 0.3 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 22, when they played out a 2-2 draw.

Manchester City's last match was on Sunday, a 2-0 win against Tottenham Hotspur Women, with Amy Turner (own goal) and Shaw getting the goals for City.

Leicester City WFC beat Everton Women 1-0 last time out, also on January 28, with Janice Cayman on the scoresheet.
