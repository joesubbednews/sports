Chertsey Town v Westfield Surrey

Isthmian League Division One – South Central | Alwyns Lane Football Ground
Saturday, 30 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Saturday's match between Chertsey Town and Westfield Surrey promises to be one for the neutrals, as two of the Isthmian League Division One South Central's most free-scoring sides go head-to-head.

Ahead of the game at the Alwyns Lane Football Ground, the Curfews and Westfield Surrey sit joint-top and fourth in the goal-scoring charts, with 85 and 61 goals respectively.

Chertsey are top of the table after 32 games, of which they have won 25 and drawn six, earning 81 points.

Westfield Surrey are five places behind Town in sixth, with 18 wins and six draws putting them on 60 points.

The Curfews are in good form in the Isthmian League Division One South Central, with four wins and two draws from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, the visitors' form is worse – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to the home side's 14.

In the last five years, Chertsey and Westfield Surrey have played each other on five occasions. Chertsey won three of them and they drew twice.

On average, the Curfews scored 1.4 goals and Westfield Surrey 0.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 26, when they played out a 0-0 draw.

Chertsey's last match was on March 23, a 3-0 win against Southall.

Westfield Surrey lost 3-0 against Raynes Park Vale last time out, also on March 23.
