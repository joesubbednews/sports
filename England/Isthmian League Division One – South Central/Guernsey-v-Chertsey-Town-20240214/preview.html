Guernsey v Chertsey Town

Isthmian League Division One – South Central
Wednesday, 14 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Chertsey Town visit Guernsey on late Wednesday on the back of eight consecutive wins in the Isthmian League Division One South Central.

It means Chertsey have picked up the maximum 24 points from their last eight games, and they face a Guernsey side who have also won their last two matches, and collected 16 points from the last possible 24.

Chertsey are top of the table after 26 games, of which they have won 21 and drawn four, earning 67 points.

Guernsey are 15 places behind Town in 16th, with seven wins and five draws putting them on 26 points.

With 71 goals in 26 games so far this season, the Curfews are the league's second-highest scorers with 2.7 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 19 goals at a rate of 0.7 per game.

The home side, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.6 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 2.0 goals per game.

In the last five years, Guernsey and Chertsey have played each other on five occasions. Chertsey won four of them and they drew once.

On average, Guernsey scored 0.8 goals and the Curfews 3.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 21, when Chertsey won 4-1 at home.

Guernsey's last match was on February 3, a 3-1 win against Ashford Town Middlesex.

Chertsey beat Badshot Lea 2-0 last time out, on February 6.
