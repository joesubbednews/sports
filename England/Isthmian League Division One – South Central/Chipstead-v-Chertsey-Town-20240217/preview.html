Chipstead v Chertsey Town

Isthmian League Division One – South Central | High Road
Saturday, 17 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Relegation candidates Chipstead face a challenge against high-flying Chertsey Town at High Road on Saturday.

Chipstead are 18th in the Isthmian League Division One – South Central table, and have picked up six wins and four draws in their 28 games to date.

The Curfews, meanwhile, are top of the standings with 67 points, having won 21 and drawn four of their first 26 matches.

Chipstead are in terrible form in the Isthmian League Division One South Central, with no wins and a draw from their last six games.

With six wins and no losses over that period, Chertsey's form is much better – they have taken 18 points from 18, compared to the hosts' one.

With 71 goals in 26 games so far this season, Town are the league's second-highest scorers with 2.7 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 19 goals at a rate of 0.7 per game.

Chipstead, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.3 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 2.1 goals per game.

In the last five years, Chipstead and Chertsey have played each other on five occasions. They won two each, and they drew once.

On average, Chipstead scored 1.8 goals and the Curfews 1.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 28, when Chertsey won 2-1 at home.

Chipstead's last match was on Tuesday, a 2-1 loss against Raynes Park Vale.

Chertsey beat Badshot Lea 2-0 last time out, on February 6.
