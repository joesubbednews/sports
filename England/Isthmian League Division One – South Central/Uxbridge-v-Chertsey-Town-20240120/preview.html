Uxbridge v Chertsey Town

Isthmian League Division One – South Central | Honeycroft
Saturday, 20 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Uxbridge and Chertsey Town come into Saturday's kick-off at Honeycroft in contrasting spells, with the away team hitting a rich vein of form while the hosts struggle.

The Reds have drawn two and lost four of the last six, while the Curfews have taken four wins and two draws.

With 58 goals in 21 games so far this season, Chertsey are the league's joint-highest scorers with 2.8 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 17 goals at a rate of 0.8 per game.

Uxbridge are also above average scorers, with 1.8 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 1.7 goals per game.

Town are top of the table after 21 games, of which they have won 16 and drawn four, earning 52 points.

The Reds are 11 places behind the Curfews in 12th, with nine wins and three draws putting them on 30 points.

In the last 10 years, Uxbridge and Chertsey have played each other on seven occasions. Uxbridge won three of them and Chertsey four.

On average, the Reds scored 1.4 goals and the Curfews 1.3 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 7, when Chertsey won 2-1 at home.

Uxbridge's last match was on January 13, a 1-1 draw against South Park.

Chertsey beat Raynes Park Vale 4-1 last time out, also on January 13.
