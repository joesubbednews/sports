Chertsey Town v Northwood

Isthmian League Division One – South Central | Alwyns Lane Football Ground
Saturday, 10 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Chertsey Town host Northwood at the Alwyns Lane Football Ground on Saturday on the back of eight consecutive wins in the Isthmian League Division One South Central.

It means Chertsey have picked up the maximum 24 points from their last eight games, and they face a Woods side who lost their last match, and have collected seven points from the last possible 24.

With 71 goals in 26 games so far this season, Chertsey are the league's joint-highest scorers with 2.7 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 19 goals at a rate of 0.7 per game.

Northwood, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.5 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 1.8 goals per game.

Town are top of the table after 26 games, of which they have won 21 and drawn four, earning 67 points.

The Woods are 16 places behind the Curfews in 17th, with seven wins and two draws putting them on 23 points.

In the last 10 years, Chertsey and Northwood have played each other on six occasions. They won three each.

On average, the Curfews scored 2.3 goals and the Woods 2.0 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 21, when Chertsey won 4-2 away.

Chertsey's last match was on Tuesday, a 2-0 win against Badshot Lea.

Northwood lost 2-1 against Westfield Surrey last time out, also on Tuesday.
