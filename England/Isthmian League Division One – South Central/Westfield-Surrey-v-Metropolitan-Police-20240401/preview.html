Westfield Surrey v Metropolitan Police

Isthmian League Division One – South Central
Monday, 1 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Metropolitan Police travel looking to secure a first win in six Isthmian League Division One – South Central games against Westfield Surrey on Monday.

Metropolitan Police have lost three and drawn two matches since they last earned three points – against Guernsey on February 17.

They face a Westfield Surrey side who have won three and drawn one over that time.

With 35 goals in 33 games so far this season, Metropolitan Police are the league's second-lowest scorers with 1.1 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 56 goals at a rate of 1.7 per game.

Westfield Surrey, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 1.4 goals per game.

The visitors are 17th in the table after 33 games, of which they have won eight and drawn eight, earning 32 points.

The home side are 11 places ahead of Metropolitan Police in sixth, with 18 wins and seven draws putting them on 61 points.

Westfield Surrey's last match was on Saturday, a 1-1 draw against Chertsey Town.

Metropolitan Police drew 2-2 with Hartley Wintney last time out, also on Saturday.
