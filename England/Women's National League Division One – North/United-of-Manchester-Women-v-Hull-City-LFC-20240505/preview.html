United of Manchester Women v Hull City LFC

Women's National League Division One – North
Sunday, 5 May 2024 @ 2.00pm

Relegation candidates United of Manchester Women face a challenge against high-flying Hull City LFC on Sunday.

United of Manchester Women are rooted to the bottom of the Women's National League Division One – North table, and have picked up just one win and two draws in their 19 games to date.

Hull City, meanwhile, are third in the standings with 38 points, having won 12 and drawn two of their first 16 matches, and are five points behind table-toppers Middlesbrough Women.

United of Manchester are in awful form in the Women's National League Division One North, with no wins and six losses from their last six games.

With five wins and a draw over that period, Hull City's form is much better – they have taken 16 points from 18, compared to the hosts' zero.

With 46 goals in 16 games so far this season, the away team are the league's highest scorers with 2.9 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 18 goals at a rate of 1.1 per game.

United of Manchester, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 0.7 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 3.4 goals per game.

Over the last two years, United of Manchester and Hull City have played each other twice. Hull City won both of them.

Their last meeting was on December 3, when Hull City won 6-0 at home.

United of Manchester's last match was on April 20, a 3-0 loss against Doncaster Belles Women.

Hull City drew 1-1 with York City LFC last time out, on April 21.
