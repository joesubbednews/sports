Durham Cestria WFC v Leeds Women

Women's National League Division One – North
Sunday, 28 January 2024 @ 2.00pm

Leeds Women face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form away against a tight Durham Cestria WFC defence on Sunday.

With 22 goals in 11 games, Leeds are the third-highest scorers in Women's National League Division One North ahead of the 2pm kick-off.

They face a Durham Cestria WFC side who have scored 19 in 12 matches, but conceded only seven goals, putting them top of the league's tightest defences.

Durham Cestria WFC are fourth in the table after 12 games, of which they have won six and drawn two, earning 20 points.

Leeds are two places behind the hosts in sixth, with six wins and one draw putting them on 19 points.

In the last five years, Durham Cestria WFC and Leeds have played each other on five occasions. They won two each, and they drew once.

On average, Durham Cestria WFC scored 1.8 goals and Leeds 1.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 17, when Leeds won 1-0 at home.

Durham Cestria WFC are in reasonable form in Women's National League Division One North, with three wins and two draws from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, the away team's form is worse – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to Durham Cestria WFC's 11.

Durham Cestria WFC's last match was on January 21, a 2-0 win against Chorley Women.

Leeds lost 3-1 against Middlesbrough Women last time out, on January 14.
