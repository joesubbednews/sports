Leeds Women v Chester-le-Street Town FC Women

Women's National League Division One – North
Sunday, 5 May 2024 @ 2.00pm

Leeds Women face Chester-le-Street Town FC Women on Sunday looking to secure a first win in six Women's National League Division One – North games.

Leeds have lost one and drawn four matches since they last earned three points – against Norton and Stockton on February 25.

They face a Chester-le-Street Town FC side who have won just one and drawn one over that time.

Chester-le-Street Town FC are 11th in the table after 18 games, of which they have won three and drawn two, earning 11 points.

Leeds are five places ahead of the visitors in sixth, with eight wins and five draws putting them on 29 points.

With 21 goals in 18 games so far this season, Chester-le-Street Town FC are the league's third-lowest scorers with 1.2 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 43 goals at a rate of 2.4 per game.

The home team, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.8 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 1.4 goals per game.

In the last three years, Leeds and Chester-le-Street Town FC have played each other on three occasions. Leeds won all of them.

Their last meeting was on February 4, when Leeds won 3-1 away.

Leeds's last match was on April 21, a 3-3 draw against Stockport County Women.

Chester-le-Street Town FC lost 3-0 against Durham Cestria WFC last time out, also on April 21.
