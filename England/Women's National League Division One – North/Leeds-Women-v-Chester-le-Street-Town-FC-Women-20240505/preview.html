Leeds Women v Chester-le-Street Town FC Women

Women's National League Division One – North
Sunday, 5 May 2024 @ 2.00pm

Leeds Women and Chester-le-Street Town FC Women both come into Sunday's early kick-off in poor form, having picked up four and one points respectively in their last six games.

Leeds have drawn four and lost two of the last six, while Chester-le-Street Town FC have taken just one draw.

With 23 goals in 20 games so far this season, Chester-le-Street Town FC are the league's second-lowest scorers with 1.1 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 51 goals at a rate of 2.5 per game.

Leeds, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 2.0 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 1.8 goals per game.

The away team are 11th in the table after 20 games, of which they have won three and drawn two, earning 11 points.

The hosts are five places ahead of Chester-le-Street Town FC in sixth, with nine wins and five draws putting them on 32 points.

In the last three years, Leeds and Chester-le-Street Town FC have played each other on three occasions. Leeds won all of them.

Their last meeting was on February 4, when Leeds won 3-1 away.

Leeds's last match was on Tuesday, a 5-1 loss against Hull City LFC.

Chester-le-Street Town FC lost 5-2 against Chorley Women last time out, on April 28.
