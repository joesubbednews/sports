Leeds Women v Hull City LFC

Women's National League Division One – North
Sunday, 10 March 2024 @ 2.00pm

Leeds Women host Hull City LFC on Sunday in the Women's National League Division One North.

In their last league match, on March 3, Leeds lost to Durham Cestria WFC 3-2 away.

Hull City won, 3-2 away at Norton and Stockton on February 4.

With 34 goals in 12 games so far this season, Hull City are the league's joint-highest scorers with 2.8 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 15 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

Leeds are also above average scorers, with 2.0 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 1.6 goals per game.

The away side are fourth in the table after 12 games, of which they have won nine and drawn one, earning 28 points.

The hosts are one place behind Hull City in fifth, with eight wins and one draw putting them on 25 points.

Leeds are in mixed form in the Women's National League Division One North, with three wins and three losses from their last six games.

With four wins and two losses over that period, Hull City's form is better – they have taken 12 points from 18, compared to Leeds's nine.

In the last three years, Leeds and Hull City have played each other on three occasions. Leeds won two of them and Hull City one.

Their last meeting was on October 8, when Hull City won 4-3 at home.
