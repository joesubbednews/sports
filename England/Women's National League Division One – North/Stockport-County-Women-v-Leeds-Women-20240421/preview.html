Stockport County Women v Leeds Women

Women's National League Division One – North
Sunday, 21 April 2024 @ 2.00pm

Stockport County Women face Leeds Women on Sunday seeking to protect their formidable unbeaten run in the Women's National League Division One North.

Stockport County are unbeaten in eight, with four wins and four draws, ahead of the 2pm kick-off.

They face a Leeds team who have won three and drawn three over the same number of games.

With 24 goals in 18 games so far this season, Stockport County are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.3 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 14 goals at a rate of 0.8 per game.

Leeds, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.7 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 1.4 goals per game.

In the last five years, Stockport County and Leeds have played each other on five occasions. Stockport County won two of them and Leeds three.

On average, Stockport County scored 1.6 goals and Leeds 2.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 20, when Leeds won 2-1 at home.

The hosts are fifth in the table after 18 games, of which they have won eight and drawn seven, earning 31 points.

The away team are one place behind Stockport County in sixth, with eight wins and four draws putting them on 28 points.

Stockport County's last match was on April 7, a 0-0 draw against Chester-le-Street Town FC Women.

Leeds drew 0-0 with Chorley Women last time out, on April 14.
