Barnsley Women v Stockport County Women

Women's National League Division One – North
Tuesday, 20 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Two of the Women's National League Division One North's meanest defences go head-to-head on Tuesday, when Stockport County Women visit Barnsley Women.

Only one side – Durham Cestria WFC – has conceded fewer goals than Stockport County to date: the away side have let in just 12 goals in 14 games.

Barnsley have conceded 15 goals in 13 games, giving them the joint-fourth tightest back line so far this season.

Barnsley are top of the table after 13 games, of which they have won nine and drawn two, earning 29 points.

Stockport County are four places behind the hosts in fifth, with six wins and five draws putting them on 23 points.

In the last five years, Barnsley and Stockport County have played each other on five occasions. Barnsley won two of them and Stockport County three.

On average, Barnsley scored 0.8 goals and Stockport County 1.4 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 6, when Barnsley won 1-0 away.

Barnsley are in mixed form in the Women's National League Division One North, with three wins and a draw from their last six games.

With three wins and two draws over that period, the away team's form is slightly better – they have taken 11 points from 18, compared to Barnsley's 10.

Barnsley's last match was on February 11, a 3-3 draw against Middlesbrough Women.

Stockport County beat York City LFC 1-0 last time out, on January 28.
