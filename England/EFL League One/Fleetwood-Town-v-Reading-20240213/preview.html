Fleetwood Town v Reading

EFL League One | Highbury Stadium
Tuesday, 13 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Reading face Fleetwood Town on Tuesday seeking to protect their long unbeaten run in EFL League One.

The Royals are unbeaten in five, with three wins and two draws, ahead of the 7.45pm kick-off.

They face a Fleetwood team who have won two and drawn one over the same number of games.

Fleetwood are 23rd in the table after 31 games, of which they have won six and drawn seven, earning 25 points.

Reading are eight places ahead of Town in 15th, with 10 wins and eight draws putting them on 38 points.

With 31 goals in 31 games so far this season, the Cod Army are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.0 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 52 goals at a rate of 1.7 per game.

The Royals, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.3 goals per game. They have conceded 1.4 goals per game.

Fleetwood's last match was on Saturday, a 2-1 loss against Lincoln City, with Promise Omochere getting the goal for the Cod Army.

Reading beat Charlton Athletic 2-0 last time out, also on Saturday, with Femi Azeez on the scoresheet.

Tuesday's match will be refereed by Simon Mather, who has taken charge of nine EFL League One games so far this season, issuing seven red cards and booking 57 players. He has awarded one penalty.

The last Fleetwood game Mather refereed was a 1-0 home win against Leyton Orient on September 30. His last Reading match was their 1-0 loss at home against Peterborough United on August 5.
