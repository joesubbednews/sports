Shrewsbury Town v Stevenage

EFL League One | The Croud Meadow
Saturday, 13 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Shrewsbury Town host Stevenage on Saturday at the Croud Meadow in EFL League One.

In their last league match, on January 1, Shrewsbury beat Fleetwood Town 3-1 at home, with goals from Cheyenne Dunkley, Jordan Shipley and Nohan Kenneh.

Stevenage lost, 2-1 away at Portsmouth, with Louis Thompson scoring their goals.

In Jamie Reid, Stevenage have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 24 appearances, to sit third in the scoring charts.

Shrewsbury's top scorers, with three goals each, are Daniel Udoh and Dunkley.

With 17 goals in 26 games so far this season, Town are the league's lowest scorers with 0.7 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 36 goals at a rate of 1.4 per game.

The Boro, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.5 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.0 goal per game.

The away side are seventh in the table after 25 games, of which they have won 12 and drawn seven, earning 43 points.

The Shrews are nine places behind Stevenage in 16th, with nine wins and three draws putting them on 30 points.

The hosts are in disappointing form in EFL League One, with two wins and four losses from their last six games.

With two wins and two draws over that period, Stevenage's form is slightly better – they have taken eight points from 18, compared to Shrewsbury's six.
