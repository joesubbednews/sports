Barnsley v Stevenage

EFL League One | Oakwell
Saturday, 23 December 2023 @ 3.00pm

Barnsley face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form at home against a tight Stevenage defence on Saturday.

With 38 goals in 20 games, Barnsley are the joint-second-highest scorers in EFL League One ahead of the 3pm kick-off at Oakwell.

They face a Boro side who have scored 32 in 22 matches, but conceded only 21 goals, putting them joint-fourth among the league's tightest defences – only Portsmouth, Peterborough United and Derby County have conceded fewer goals.

Stevenage are fourth in the table after 22 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn seven, earning 40 points.

Barnsley are three places behind the Boro in seventh, with 10 wins and five draws putting them on 35 points.

In Jamie Reid, the visitors have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 13 goals in 21 appearances, to sit third in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 125 minutes is quicker than that of Devante Cole, the Tykes's top scorer with a goal every 138 minutes, and a total of 12 goals in 20 games.

The home side are in mixed form in EFL League One, with two wins and three draws from their last six games.

With four wins and two draws over that period, Stevenage's form is better – they have taken 14 points from 18, compared to Barnsley's nine.

Barnsley's last match was on December 16, a 1-1 draw against Charlton Athletic, with Adam Phillips getting the goal for the Tykes.

Stevenage drew 1-1 with Exeter City last time out, also on December 16, with Carl Piergianni on the scoresheet.
