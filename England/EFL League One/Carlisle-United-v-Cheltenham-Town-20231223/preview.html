Carlisle United v Cheltenham Town

EFL League One | Brunton Park
Saturday, 23 December 2023 @ 3.00pm

Saturday's match at Brunton Park sees two relegation candidates play each other, as 22nd-ranked Carlisle United host bottom of the table Cheltenham Town.

Carlisle have picked up 16 points from their first 16 EFL League One games, with three wins and seven draws.

That is three points more than the Robins have collected, having won three and drawn four.

Carlisle are in terrible form in EFL League One, with no wins and two draws from their last six games.

With two wins and two draws over that period, Cheltenham's form is much better – they have taken eight points from 18, compared to United's two.

In the last five years, Carlisle and Cheltenham have played each other on five occasions. Carlisle won one of them, Cheltenham three, and they drew once.

On average, Carlisle scored 0.8 goals and the Robins 1.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on April 27 2021, when they played out a 1-1 draw.

With 12 goals in 20 games so far this season, Town are the league's lowest scorers with 0.6 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 30 goals at a rate of 1.5 per game.

The hosts are also below average scorers, with 0.8 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

Carlisle's last match was on December 16, a 2-2 draw against Northampton Town, with Jack Armer and Ryan Edmondson getting the goals for Carlisle.

Cheltenham lost 2-1 against Leyton Orient last time out, also on December 16, with William Neil Goodwin on the scoresheet.
