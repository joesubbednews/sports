Port Vale v Portsmouth

EFL League One | Vale Park
Saturday, 27 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Port Vale host Portsmouth on Saturday at Vale Park in EFL League One.

In their last league match, on January 6, Vale drew with Charlton Athletic 3-3 at home, with goals from Ethan Chislett, Gavin Massey and Uche Ikpeazu.

Portsmouth won, 1-0 away at Fleetwood Town on January 20, with Abu Kamara scoring their goals.

Portsmouth are top of the table after 28 games, of which they have won 16 and drawn eight, earning 56 points.

Vale are 17 places behind Pompey in 18th, with eight wins and six draws putting them on 30 points.

With Will Norris between the sticks, the visitors can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept 13 clean sheets in his 28 appearances this season, and no 'keeper has prevented the opposition scoring more often in EFL League One.

In the Valiants's net, Connor Ripley has seven clean sheets in 25 games. He has conceded a goal every 66 minutes, 70% more often than the 110 minutes between goals for Norris.

With 43 goals in 28 games so far this season, Portsmouth are scoring more than average in the league with 1.5 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 25 goals at a rate of 0.9 per game.

The home side, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

Vale are in mixed form in EFL League One, with two wins and two draws from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, Portsmouth's form is slightly worse – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to Vale's eight.

In the last three years, Vale and Portsmouth have played each other on three occasions. Portsmouth won two of them and they drew once.

Their last meeting was on October 7, when Portsmouth won 2-0 at home.
