Billericay Town Women v Oxford United Women

Women's National League Premier Division – South
Sunday, 31 March 2024 @ 2.00pm

Relegation candidates Billericay Town Women face a challenge against high-flying Oxford United Women on Sunday.

Billericay Town Women are ninth in the Women's National League Premier Division – South table, and have picked up four wins and five draws in their 19 games to date.

Oxford United, meanwhile, are fourth in the standings with 37 points, having won 12 and drawn one, and are 14 points behind table-toppers Portsmouth Women.

With 42 goals in 19 games so far this season, Oxford United are the league's joint-third-highest scorers with 2.2 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 25 goals at a rate of 1.3 per game.

Billericay Town, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.9 goals per game. They have conceded 2.4 goals per game.

The home side are in bad form in the Women's National League Premier Division South, with one win and a draw from their last six games.

With three wins and three losses over that period, the visitors' form is better – they have taken nine points from 18, compared to Billericay Town's four.

In the last five years, Billericay Town and Oxford United have played each other on four occasions. Oxford United won three of them and they drew once.

On average, Billericay Town scored 0.8 goals and Oxford United 3.5 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on December 17, when Oxford United won 8-0 at home.

Billericay Town's last match was on Sunday, a 4-2 loss against Cheltenham Town LFC.

Oxford United beat London Bees Women 3-1 last time out, also on Sunday.
