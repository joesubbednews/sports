Oxford United Women v Milton Keynes Dons Women

Women's National League Premier Division – South
Sunday, 21 April 2024 @ 2.00pm

Oxford United Women face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form at home against a tight Milton Keynes Dons Women defence on Sunday.

With 44 goals in 20 games, Oxford United are the third-highest scorers in the Women's National League Premier Division South ahead of the 2pm kick-off.

They face a Milton Keynes Dons side who have scored 31 in 19 matches, but conceded only 23 goals, putting them fourth among the league's tightest defences – only Portsmouth Women, C and K Basildon and Ipswich Town Women have conceded fewer goals.

Oxford United are fourth in the table after 20 games, of which they have won 13 and drawn one, earning 40 points.

Milton Keynes Dons are two places behind the home team in sixth, with nine wins and four draws putting them on 31 points.

In the last five years, Oxford United and Milton Keynes Dons have played each other on five occasions. Oxford United won three of them, Milton Keynes Dons one, and they drew once.

On average, Oxford United scored 2.4 goals and Milton Keynes Dons 1.0 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 20, when they played out a 2-2 draw.

Oxford United are in reasonable form in the Women's National League Premier Division South, with four wins and two losses from their last six games.

With three wins and two draws over that period, the visitors' form is slightly worse – they have taken 11 points from 18, compared to Oxford United's 12.

Oxford United's last match was on March 31, a 2-0 win against Billericay Town Women.

Milton Keynes Dons drew 0-0 with Portsmouth Women last time out, also on March 31.
