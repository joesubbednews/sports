Norwich City v Cambridge City Women

Women's National League Division One – South East
Tuesday, 20 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Mid-season relegation candidates Cambridge City Women face a challenge away against high-flying Norwich City on Tuesday.

Cambridge City Women are 11th in the Women's National League Division One – South East table, and have picked up just one win and three draws in their 14 games to date.

Norwich, meanwhile, are second in the standings with 33 points, having won 10 and drawn three of their first 13 matches, and are six points behind table-toppers AFC Wimbledon Women.

With 40 goals in 13 games so far this season, Norwich are the league's second-highest scorers with 3.1 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 13 goals at a rate of 1.0 per game.

Cambridge City, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.2 goals per game, compared to a league average of 2.1. They have conceded 3.2 goals per game.

City are in fantastic form in the Women's National League Division One South East, with five wins and a draw from their last six games.

With a win and a draw over that period, the away team's form is much worse – they have taken four points from 18, compared to the hosts' 16.

In the last five years, Norwich and Cambridge City have played each other on five occasions. Norwich won all of them.

On average, Norwich scored 2.4 goals and Cambridge City 0.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 5, when Norwich won 4-0 away.

Norwich's last match was on February 4, a 2-1 win against London Seaward Women.

Cambridge City lost 2-1 against Hounslow WFC last time out, on January 28.
