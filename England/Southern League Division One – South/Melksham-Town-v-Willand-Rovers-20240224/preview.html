Melksham Town v Willand Rovers

Southern League Division One – South
Saturday, 24 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Melksham Town welcome Willand Rovers on Saturday looking to pick up points to end their four-game losing streak.

Melksham's struggles have left them with just three points from their last six Southern League Division One – South matches, while their opponents have earned six from a possible 18.

With 33 goals in 23 games so far this season, Melksham are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.4 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 42 goals at a rate of 1.8 per game.

Willand, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.7 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have also conceded 1.7 goals per game.

Town are 15th in the table after 23 games, of which they have won six and drawn eight, earning 26 points.

Rovers are six places ahead of the hosts in ninth, with 10 wins and five draws putting them on 35 points.

In the last five years, Melksham and Willand have played each other on five occasions. They won two each, and they drew once.

On average, Melksham scored 1.6 goals and Willand 1.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on November 18, when they played out a 0-0 draw.

Melksham's last match was on February 10, a 1-0 loss against Tavistock.

Willand drew 1-1 with Cribbs last time out, on Tuesday.
