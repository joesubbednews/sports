Evesham United v Frome Town

Southern League Division One – South | The Spiers & Hartwell Jubilee Stadium
Saturday, 23 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Frome Town face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form away against a tight Evesham United defence on Saturday.

With 58 goals in 28 games, Frome are the second-highest scorers in the Southern League Division One South ahead of the 3pm kick-off at the Spiers & Hartwell Jubilee Stadium.

They face an Evesham side who have scored 42 in 28 matches, but conceded only 32 goals, putting them joint-second among the league's tightest defences – only Hamworthy United have conceded fewer goals.

Frome are second in the table after 28 games, of which they have won 17 and drawn six, earning 57 points.

Evesham are three places behind Town in fifth, with 10 wins and 11 draws putting them on 41 points.

In the last 10 years, Evesham and Frome have played each other on six occasions. Evesham won one of them, Frome three, and they drew twice.

On average, Evesham scored 1.2 goals and Frome 1.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 26, when Frome won 3-2 at home.

United are in mixed form in the Southern League Division One South, with two wins and three draws from their last six games.

With four wins and two draws over that period, the visitors' form is better – they have taken 14 points from 18, compared to the home team's nine.

Evesham's last match was on March 2, a 2-0 win against Bideford.

Frome beat Yate Town 2-0 last time out, on March 16.
