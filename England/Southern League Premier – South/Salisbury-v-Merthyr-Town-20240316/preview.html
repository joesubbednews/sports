Salisbury v Merthyr Town

Southern League Premier – South | The Raymond McEnhill Stadium
Saturday, 16 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Merthyr Town face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form away against a tight Salisbury defence on Saturday.

With 67 goals in 32 games, Merthyr are the second-highest scorers in the Southern League Premier South ahead of the 3pm kick-off at the Raymond McEnhill Stadium.

They face a Salisbury side who have scored 59 in 33 matches, but conceded only 37 goals, putting them joint-fourth among the league's tightest defences – only Chesham United, Gosport Borough and AFC Totton have conceded fewer goals.

Salisbury are second in the table after 33 games, of which they have won 19 and drawn eight, earning 65 points.

Merthyr are three places behind the hosts in fifth, with 16 wins and four draws putting them on 52 points.

In the last 10 years, Salisbury and Merthyr have played each other on seven occasions. Salisbury won three of them and Merthyr four.

On average, Salisbury scored 1.1 goals and Merthyr 2.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on December 2, when Merthyr won 2-1 at home.

Salisbury are in reasonable form in the Southern League Premier South, with three wins and two draws from their last six games.

With a win and a draw over that period, Town's form is much worse – they have taken four points from 18, compared to Salisbury's 11.

Salisbury's last match was on March 9, a 3-1 win against Chesham United.

Merthyr lost 3-2 against Bracknell Town last time out, also on March 9.
