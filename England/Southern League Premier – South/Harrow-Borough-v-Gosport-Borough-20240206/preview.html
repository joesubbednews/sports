Harrow Borough v Gosport Borough

Southern League Premier – South | Earlsmead Stadium
Tuesday, 6 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Mid-season relegation candidates Harrow Borough face a challenge against high-flying Gosport Borough at the Earlsmead Stadium on Tuesday.

Harrow Borough are rooted to the bottom of the Southern League Premier – South table, and have picked up three wins and six draws in their 27 games to date.

Gosport, meanwhile, are third in the standings with 51 points, having won 15 and drawn six of their first 26 matches, and are 10 points behind table-toppers Chesham United.

Harrow are in terrible form in the Southern League Premier South, with no wins and a draw from their last six games.

With two wins and three draws over that period, Gosport's form is much better – they have taken nine points from 18, compared to Harrow Borough's one.

In the last 10 years, Harrow and Gosport have played each other on seven occasions. They won three each, and they drew once.

On average, Harrow scored 1.1 goals and Gosport 1.3 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on March 25, when Gosport won 2-0 away.

With 31 goals in 27 games so far this season, the home side are the league's second-lowest scorers with 1.1 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 64 goals at a rate of 2.4 per game.

Gosport Borough, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 1.0 goal per game.

Harrow's last match was on January 27, a 1-1 draw against Hayes and Yeading United.

Gosport beat Merthyr Town 2-1 last time out, on Saturday.
