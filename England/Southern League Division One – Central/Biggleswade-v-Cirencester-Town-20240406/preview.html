Biggleswade v Cirencester Town

Southern League Division One – Central
Saturday, 6 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Cirencester Town travel to Biggleswade on Saturday in the Southern League Division One Central.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a win in their last match, having beaten AFC Kempston Rovers 3-1 away.

Biggleswade also won their last match, 3-1 against Stotfold.

With 50 goals in 27 games so far this season, Biggleswade are scoring more than average in the league with 1.9 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 43 goals at a rate of 1.6 per game.

Cirencester, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.7 goals per game. They have conceded 1.3 goals per game.

The hosts are in reasonable form in the Southern League Division One Central, with four wins and two losses from their last six games.

With a win and two draws over that period, Town's form is much worse – they have taken five points from 18, compared to Biggleswade's 12.

The away team are sixth in the table after 31 games, of which they have won 12 and drawn eight, earning 44 points.

Biggleswade are three places behind Cirencester in ninth, with 12 wins and four draws putting them on 40 points.

In the last three years, Biggleswade and Cirencester have played each other on three occasions. Biggleswade won two of them and Cirencester one.

Their last meeting was on December 2, when Biggleswade won 2-1 away.
