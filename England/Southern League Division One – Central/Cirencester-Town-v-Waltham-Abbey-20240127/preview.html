Cirencester Town v Waltham Abbey

Southern League Division One – Central | The Corinium Stadium
Saturday, 27 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Waltham Abbey face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form away against a tight Cirencester Town defence on Saturday.

With 43 goals in 22 games, Waltham Abbey are the joint-third-highest scorers in Southern League Division One Central ahead of the 3pm kick-off at the Corinium Stadium.

They face a Cirencester side who have scored 37 in 21 matches, but conceded only 24 goals, putting them second among the league's tightest defences – only Bedford Town have conceded fewer goals.

Waltham Abbey are fifth in the table after 22 games, of which they have won 10 and drawn five, earning 35 points.

Cirencester are one place behind the visitors in sixth, with nine wins and five draws putting them on 32 points.

Town are in mixed form in Southern League Division One Central, with three wins and a draw from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, Waltham Abbey's form is worse – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to the home team's 10.

In the last three years, Cirencester and Waltham Abbey have played each other on three occasions. Waltham Abbey won one of them and they drew the other.

Their last meeting was on October 28, when Waltham Abbey won 1-0 at home.

Cirencester's last match was on January 13, a 1-0 loss against Kidlington.

Waltham Abbey drew 1-1 with Biggleswade last time out, on January 20.
