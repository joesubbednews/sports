Cirencester Town v Biggleswade Town

Southern League Division One – Central | The Corinium Stadium
Saturday, 9 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Two of the Southern League Division One Central's meanest defences go head-to-head at the Corinium Stadium on Saturday, when Biggleswade Town visit Cirencester Town.

Only one side – Bedford Town – has conceded fewer goals than Biggleswade to date: the away side have let in just 29 goals in 24 games.

Cirencester have conceded 31 goals in 27 games, giving them the third tightest back line so far this season.

Biggleswade are second in the table after 24 games, of which they have won 13 and drawn six, earning 45 points.

Cirencester are five places behind Biggleswade Town in seventh, with 11 wins and seven draws putting them on 40 points.

In the last 10 years, Cirencester and Biggleswade have played each other on eight occasions. Cirencester won two of them, Biggleswade four, and they drew twice.

On average, Cirencester scored 0.5 goals and Biggleswade 1.4 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on December 3 2022, when Biggleswade won 1-0 away.

Cirencester Town are in mixed form in the Southern League Division One Central, with two wins and two draws from their last six games.

With three wins and two draws over that period, the away side's form is better – they have taken 11 points from 18, compared to the hosts' eight.

Cirencester's last match was on March 2, a 1-0 loss against Ware.

Biggleswade beat Kidlington 4-0 last time out, on February 24.
