Waltham Abbey v Biggleswade

Southern League Division One – Central | Capershotts
Saturday, 20 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Waltham Abbey host Biggleswade in Saturday's match at Capershotts looking to bounce back from defeat last time out in Southern League Division One Central.

Waltham Abbey, who sit fourth in the league after 21 games, fell to a 4-3 away defeat to Hadley on January 13.

They face a Biggleswade side who also lost their last match, a 2-1 defeat to Biggleswade Town, and who sit 12th in the table.

With 42 goals in 21 games so far this season, Waltham Abbey are the league's joint-second-highest scorers with 2.0 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 34 goals at a rate of 1.6 per game.

Biggleswade are also above average scorers, with 1.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.8. They have conceded 1.8 goals per game.

In the last five years, Waltham Abbey and Biggleswade have played each other on five occasions. Waltham Abbey won two of them and Biggleswade three.

On average, Waltham Abbey scored 1.2 goals and Biggleswade 1.0 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 30, when Waltham Abbey won 1-0 away.

The hosts are in mixed form in Southern League Division One Central, with two wins and a draw from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, the away side's form is better – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to Waltham Abbey's seven.
