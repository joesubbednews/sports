Biggleswade Town v Kings Langley

Southern League Division One – Central | The Carlsberg Stadium
Saturday, 23 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Saturday's match between Biggleswade Town and Kings Langley promises to be one for the neutrals, as two of the Southern League Division One Central's most free-scoring sides go head-to-head.

Ahead of the game at the Carlsberg Stadium, Biggleswade and Kings sit joint-fourth and second in the goal-scoring charts, with 54 and 57 goals respectively.

Biggleswade are second in the table after 25 games, of which they have won 14 and drawn six, earning 48 points.

Kings Langley are seven places behind Town in ninth, with 10 wins and nine draws putting them on 39 points.

In the last 10 years, Biggleswade and Kings Langley have played each other on six occasions. They won two each, and they drew twice.

On average, Biggleswade scored 1.8 goals and Kings 2.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 26, when they played out a 2-2 draw.

The hosts are in reasonable form in the Southern League Division One Central, with three wins and two draws from their last six games.

With two wins and a draw over that period, Kings's form is worse – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to Biggleswade's 11.

Biggleswade's last match was on March 9, a 4-1 win against Cirencester Town.

Kings Langley beat AFC Dunstable 4-2 last time out, on March 16.
