Biggleswade Town v Cirencester Town

Southern League Division One – Central | The Carlsberg Stadium
Saturday, 20 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Two of Southern League Division One Central's meanest defences go head-to-head at the Carlsberg Stadium on Saturday, when Cirencester Town visit Biggleswade Town.

Only one side – Bedford Town – has conceded fewer goals than Cirencester to date: the away side have let in just 24 goals in 21 games.

Biggleswade have conceded 25 goals in 19 games, giving them the third tightest back line so far this season.

Biggleswade are third in the table after 19 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn four, earning 37 points.

Cirencester are three places behind Biggleswade Town in sixth, with nine wins and five draws putting them on 32 points.

In the last 10 years, Biggleswade and Cirencester have played each other on eight occasions. Biggleswade won four of them, Cirencester two, and they drew twice.

On average, Biggleswade scored 1.4 goals and Cirencester 0.5 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on December 3 2022, when Biggleswade won 1-0 away.

The hosts are in good form in Southern League Division One Central, with four wins and two draws from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, Cirencester Town's form is worse – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to Biggleswade's 14.

Biggleswade's last match was on January 13, a 2-1 win against Biggleswade.

Cirencester lost 1-0 against Kidlington last time out, also on January 13.
