West Brom WFC v Fylde Women

Women's National League Premier Division – North | The Valley Stadium
Sunday, 31 March 2024 @ 3.00pm

Struggling West Brom WFC face Fylde Women at the Valley Stadium on Sunday looking to build on a win in their last league outing.

After securing all three points with a 0-2 victory over Wolverhampton Wanderers Women on March 22, West Brom WFC sit fourth in the Women's National League Premier Division North.

They travel to play a Fylde side 12th in the standings, who were held in their last match, 0-0 against Derby County Women, on March 24.

West Brom WFC are in reasonable form in the Women's National League Premier Division North, with three wins and two draws from their last six games.

With no wins and two draws over that period, Fylde's form is much worse – they have taken two points from 18, compared to the hosts' 11.

In the last 10 years, West Brom WFC and Fylde have played each other on six occasions. West Brom WFC won two of them, Fylde one, and they drew three times.

On average, West Brom WFC scored 1.7 goals and Fylde 1.7 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 8, when they played out a 2-2 draw.

With 14 goals in 16 games so far this season, the away side are the league's third-lowest scorers with 0.9 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 52 goals at a rate of 3.2 per game.

West Brom WFC are also below average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.
