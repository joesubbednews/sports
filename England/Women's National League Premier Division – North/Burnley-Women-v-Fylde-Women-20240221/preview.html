Burnley Women v Fylde Women

Women's National League Premier Division – North
Wednesday, 21 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Struggling Burnley Women face Fylde Women on Wednesday looking to build on a win in their last league outing.

After securing all three points with a 0-3 victory over Newcastle United Women on February 11, Burnley sit third in the Women's National League Premier Division North.

They travel to play a Fylde side 12th in the standings, who were held in their last match, 0-0 against Liverpool Feds.

Burnley are in mixed form in the Women's National League Premier Division North, with two wins and two draws from their last six games.

With no wins and two draws over that period, Fylde's form is much worse – they have taken two points from 18, compared to the hosts' eight.

With 13 goals in 14 games so far this season, the away team are the league's third-lowest scorers with 0.9 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 50 goals at a rate of 3.6 per game.

Burnley, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 2.2 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.7. They have conceded 1.1 goals per game.

In the last 10 years, Burnley and Fylde have played each other on seven occasions. Burnley won five of them, Fylde one, and they drew once.

On average, Burnley scored 2.9 goals and Fylde 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 7, when Burnley won 3-0 away.
