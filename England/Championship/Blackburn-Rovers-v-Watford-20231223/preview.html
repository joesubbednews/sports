Blackburn Rovers v Watford

Championship | Ewood Park
Saturday, 23 December 2023 @ 3.00pm

Watford travel to Blackburn Rovers on Saturday in the Championship.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a win in their last match, having beaten Preston North End 5-1 away, with two goals from Vakoun Issouf Bayo, one from Edo Kayembe, one from Matheus Martins Silva dos Santos and one from Ismael Koné.

Blackburn, meanwhile, lost their last match, 4-0 against Southampton.

In Samuel Szmodics, Blackburn have the league's sharpest shooter so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 22 appearances.

His goal rate of one every 138 minutes is slightly quicker than that of Mileta Rajovic, Watford's top scorer with a goal every 149 minutes, and a total of seven goals in 19 games.

With 35 goals in 22 games so far this season, the Hornets are scoring more than average in the league with 1.6 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 27 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

Rovers are also above average scorers, with 1.5 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.4. They have conceded 1.7 goals per game.

In the last 10 years, Blackburn and Watford have played each other on eight occasions. Blackburn won two of them, Watford three, and they drew three times.

On average, Blackburn scored 1.5 goals and the Hornets 1.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 27, when Blackburn won 1-0 away.

The visitors are ninth in the table after 22 games, of which they have won eight and drawn seven, earning 31 points.

The home side are two places behind Watford in 11th, with 10 wins and one draw putting them on the same number of points.

Blackburn are in mixed form in the Championship, with three wins and three losses from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, Watford's form is slightly better – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to Blackburn's nine.

Saturday's match will be refereed by Dean Whitestone, who has taken charge of 14 Championship games so far this season, issuing one red card and booking 45 players. He has awarded one penalty.

The last Blackburn game Whitestone refereed was a 2-1 home win against West Bromwich Albion on August 5. His last Watford match was their 1-0 win at home against Sheffield Wednesday on October 21.
