AFC Wimbledon v Barrow

EFL League Two | The Cherry Red Records Stadium
Saturday, 10 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Two of EFL League Two's meanest defences go head-to-head at the Cherry Red Records Stadium on Saturday, when Barrow visit AFC Wimbledon.

Only two sides – Mansfield Town and Stockport County – have conceded fewer goals than Barrow to date: the away side have let in just 30 goals in 30 games.

The Dons have conceded 34 goals in 29 games, giving them the fourth tightest back line so far this season.

Key to the Bluebirds' home form has been Paul Farman, who has allowed 0.96 goals past him per 90 minutes, compared to 1.1 for Alex Bass in the opposite net.

Barrow are third in the table after 30 games, of which they have won 14 and drawn 11, earning 53 points.

Wimbledon are six places behind the Bluebirds in ninth, with 11 wins and 10 draws putting them on 43 points.

In the last five years, Wimbledon and Barrow have played each other on four occasions. Barrow won two of them and they drew twice.

On average, the Dons scored 0.2 goals and the Bluebirds 0.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 21, when they played out a 0-0 draw.

In Ali Al-Hamadi, the Dons have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 13 goals in 23 appearances, to sit eighth in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 152 minutes is slightly quicker than that of Ben Whitfield, the away side's top scorer with a goal every 126 minutes, and a total of seven goals in 20 games.

The hosts are in mixed form in EFL League Two, with two wins and two draws from their last six games.

And also with two wins and two draws over that period, Barrow's form is identical – they have both taken eight points from 18.

Wimbledon's last match was on February 3, a 0-0 draw against Bradford City.

Barrow beat Milton Keynes Dons 1-0 last time out, also on February 3, with Cole Stockton on the scoresheet.
