Wrexham v AFC Wimbledon

EFL League Two | Racecourse Ground
Saturday, 13 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Wrexham face a challenge to maintain their high-scoring form at home against a tight AFC Wimbledon defence on Saturday.

With 52 goals in 25 games, Wrexham are the third-highest scorers in EFL League Two ahead of the 3pm kick-off at Racecourse Ground.

They face a Dons side who have scored 41 in 25 matches, but conceded only 28 goals, putting them fourth among the league's tightest defences – only Stockport County, Mansfield Town and Barrow have conceded fewer goals.

Wrexham are third in the table after 25 games, of which they have won 14 and drawn seven, earning 49 points.

Wimbledon are four places behind the Dragons in seventh, with 10 wins and nine draws putting them on 39 points.

In Alex Bass, the Dons can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept nine clean sheets in his 25 appearances this season in EFL League Two.

In the home team's net, Arthur Okonkwo has seven clean sheets in 15 games.

Wrexham are in good form in EFL League Two, with four wins and a draw from their last six games.

With three wins and two draws over that period, the visitors' form is slightly worse – they have taken 11 points from 18, compared to Wrexham's 13.

Wrexham's last match was on January 1, a 4-1 win against Barrow, with Steven Fletcher (three) and Paul Philip Mullin getting the goals for the Dragons.

Wimbledon drew 1-1 with Forest Green Rovers last time out, also on January 1, with Ali Al-Hamadi on the scoresheet.
