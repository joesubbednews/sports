Notts County v Sutton United

EFL League Two | Meadow Lane
Saturday, 6 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Sutton United travel to Notts County on Saturday in EFL League Two.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a draw in their last match, having tied with Newport County 1-1 at home, with a goal from Olusanya Temi Fadahunsi.

County, meanwhile, lost their last match, 4-2 against Tranmere Rovers, with their goals scored by Aaron Evans Nemane and Macaulay Langstaff.

With 55 goals in 26 games so far this season, County are the league's highest scorers with 2.1 goals per game. But they are conceding more than average too, letting in 47 goals at a rate of 1.8 per game.

Sutton, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 2.1 goals per game.

United are 23rd in the table after 25 games, of which they have won four and drawn six, earning 18 points.

The hosts are 18 places ahead of the Us in fifth, with 13 wins and three draws putting them on 42 points.

In Langstaff, County have the league's sharpest shooter so far this season. He has notched 19 goals in 26 appearances.

His goal rate of one every 119 minutes is quicker than that of Harry Smith, the away team's top scorer with a goal every 222 minutes, and a total of seven goals in 20 games.

County are in disappointing form in EFL League Two, with two wins and four losses from their last six games.

With a win and two draws over that period, Sutton's form is slightly worse – they have taken five points from 18, compared to the home side's six.
