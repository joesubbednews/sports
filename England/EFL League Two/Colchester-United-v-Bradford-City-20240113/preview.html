Colchester United v Bradford City

EFL League Two | JobServe Community Stadium
Saturday, 13 January 2024 @ 3.00pm

Colchester United are on a poor run ahead of hosting Bradford City at the JobServe Community Stadium on Saturday, with just four points collected from their last six games.

The U's have picked up one win and one draw in their last six EFL League Two games, and face a Bantams side whose last six games have brought two wins and two draws.

Colchester are 22nd in the table after 27 games, of which they have won seven and drawn three, earning 24 points.

Bradford are eight places ahead of United in 14th, with nine wins and seven draws putting them on 34 points.

In the last 10 years, Colchester and Bradford have played each other on 13 occasions. They won four each, and they drew five times.

On average, the U's scored 0.8 goals and the Bantams 0.8 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 12, when Bradford won 2-1 at home.

With 33 goals in 26 games so far this season, City are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.3 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 35 goals at a rate of 1.3 per game.

The U's are also below average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 2.0 goals per game.

Colchester's last match was on January 6, a 2-2 draw against Swindon Town, with Bradley Ihionvien and Matt Jay getting the goals for the U's.

Bradford lost 4-2 against Crawley Town last time out, also on January 6, with Corey Addai (own goal) and Liam Ridehalgh on the scoresheet.

Saturday's match will be refereed by Scott Simpson, who has taken charge of eight EFL League Two games so far this season, issuing four red cards and booking 49 players. He has awarded six penalties.

The last Bradford game Simpson refereed was the 1-0 win away at AFC Wimbledon on October 14. He is yet to oversee a match featuring Colchester this season.
