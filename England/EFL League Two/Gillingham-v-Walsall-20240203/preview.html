Gillingham v Walsall

EFL League Two | MEMS Priestfield Stadium
Saturday, 3 February 2024 @ 3.00pm

Gillingham host Walsall on Saturday at the MEMS Priestfield Stadium in EFL League Two.

In their last league match, on January 27, Gillingham lost to Milton Keynes Dons 2-1 away, with their goal scored by Joshua James Walker.

Walsall drew, 1-1 at home against Sutton United, with Donervon Joseph Daniels scoring their goals.

In Jake Turner, Gillingham can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept 10 clean sheets in his 28 appearances this season, and no 'keeper has prevented the opposition scoring more often in EFL League Two.

In Walsall's net, Owen Evans has three clean sheets in 20 games. He has conceded a goal every 65 minutes, 10% more often than the 74 minutes between goals for Turner.

In the last 10 years, Gillingham and Walsall have played each other on 14 occasions. Gillingham won two of them, Walsall six, and they drew six times.

On average, the Gills scored 0.8 goals and the Saddlers 1.4 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 14, when Walsall won 4-1 at home.

With 25 goals in 28 games so far this season, the Gills are the league's lowest scorers with 0.9 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 34 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

The Saddlers, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.5 goals per game. They have also conceded 1.5 goals per game.

The home team are ninth in the table after 28 games, of which they have won 13 and drawn three, earning 42 points.

The visitors are four places behind Gillingham in 13th, with 10 wins and seven draws putting them on 37 points.

Gillingham are in mixed form in EFL League Two, with three wins and a draw from their last six games.

And also with three wins and a draw over that period, Walsall's form is identical – they have both taken 10 points from 18.
