Sutton United v Wrexham

EFL League Two | VBS Community Stadium
Tuesday, 13 February 2024 @ 7.45pm

Wrexham are strong favourites to take all three points despite Sutton United's home advantage in Tuesday's late match at the VBS Community Stadium.

*Betting Company* are offering odds of 1.78 on Wrexham sealing the win, with the visitors sitting fifth in EFL League Two table.

Sutton, who are 23rd in the league and 30 points behind the Dragons, are priced at 3.55 to win. A draw is set at 3.6.

With 55 goals in 29 games so far this season, Wrexham are scoring more than average in the league with 1.9 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 41 goals at a rate of 1.4 per game.

Sutton, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.5. They have conceded 1.9 goals per game.

In the last 10 years, Sutton and Wrexham have played each other on 11 occasions. Sutton won three of them, Wrexham four, and they drew four times.

On average, the Us scored 1.0 goal and the Dragons 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 24, when Wrexham won 2-1 at home.

United are in bad form in EFL League Two, with no wins and four draws from their last six games.

With two wins and four losses over that period, the Dragons' form is slightly better – they have taken six points from 18, compared to the Us' four.

In Elliott Lee, the away team have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 13 goals in 29 appearances, to sit eighth in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 192 minutes is quicker than that of Harry Smith, the hosts' top scorer with a goal every 233 minutes, and a total of seven goals in 22 games.

Sutton's last match was on Saturday, a 1-0 loss against Morecambe.

Wrexham lost 1-0 against Bradford City last time out, also on Saturday.

Tuesday's match will be refereed by Daniel Middleton, who has taken charge of eight EFL League Two games so far this season, issuing one red card and booking 31 players. He has awarded two penalties.

The last Wrexham game Middleton refereed was the 1-1 draw away at Barrow on August 26. He is yet to oversee a match featuring Sutton this season.
