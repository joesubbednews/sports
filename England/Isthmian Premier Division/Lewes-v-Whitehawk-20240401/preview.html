Lewes v Whitehawk

Isthmian Premier Division | The Dripping Pan
Monday, 1 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Whitehawk travel to Lewes on Monday in the Isthmian Premier Division.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a defeat in their last match, having lost to Canvey Island 2-0 at home.

Lewes also lost their last match, 3-1 against Folkestone Invicta.

With 47 goals in 33 games so far this season, Whitehawk are scoring at below the league average rate with 1.4 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 49 goals at a rate of 1.5 per game.

Lewes, meanwhile, are average scorers, with 1.6 goals per game. They have conceded 1.7 goals per game.

The Hawks are 16th in the table after 33 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn 11, earning 44 points.

The home team are five places ahead of the visitors in 11th, with 15 wins and six draws putting them on 51 points.

Lewes are in mixed form in the Isthmian Premier Division, with two wins and a draw from their last six games.

With two wins and two draws over that period, Whitehawk's form is slightly better – they have taken eight points from 18, compared to Lewes's seven.

Over the last two years, Lewes and Whitehawk have played each other twice. They won one each.

Their last meeting was on December 26, when Lewes won 2-1 away.
