Wingate and Finchley v Haringey Borough

Isthmian Premier Division | Harry Abrahams Stadium
Saturday, 13 April 2024 @ 3.00pm

Relegation candidates Haringey Borough face a challenge away against high-flying Wingate and Finchley at the Harry Abrahams Stadium on Saturday.

Haringey Borough are 20th in the Isthmian Premier Division table, and have picked up five wins and nine draws in their 38 games to date.

The Blues, meanwhile, are third in the standings with 68 points, having won 21 and drawn five of their first 37 matches, and are 20 points behind table-toppers AFC Hornchurch.

With 28 goals in 38 games so far this season, Haringey are the league's lowest scorers with 0.7 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 81 goals at a rate of 2.1 per game.

Wingate & Finchley, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.6. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

The Blues are in fantastic form in the Isthmian Premier Division, with five wins and one loss from their last six games.

With a win and five losses over that period, Borough's form is much worse – they have taken three points from 18, compared to the home side's 15.

In the last 10 years, Wingate & Finchley and Haringey have played each other on 10 occasions. Wingate & Finchley won three of them, Haringey five, and they drew twice.

On average, the Blues scored 0.8 goals and Haringey 1.5 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on August 15, when Wingate & Finchley won 2-1 away.

Wingate & Finchley's last match was on April 6, a 2-0 win against Concord Rangers.

Haringey lost 6-0 against Billericay Town last time out, also on April 6.
