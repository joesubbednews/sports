Ceuta v CD Atlético Baleares

Primera Division RFEF Group 2
Sunday, 21 April 2024 @ 11.00am

Ceuta and CD Atlético Baleares come into Sunday's early kick-off in contrasting spells, with the home team hitting a rich vein of form while the visitors struggle.

Ceuta have won five and drawn one of the last six, while Atlético Baleares have taken two draws and four losses.

Atlético Baleares are 19th in the table after 32 games, of which they have won five and drawn eight, earning 23 points.

Ceuta are 14 places ahead of the visitors in fifth, with 14 wins and 11 draws putting them on 53 points.

With 16 goals in 32 games so far this season, Atlético Baleares are the league's lowest scorers with 0.5 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 50 goals at a rate of 1.6 per game.

The home side, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.4 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.1. They have conceded 1.0 goal per game.

In Pedro López Galisteo, Ceuta can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept nine clean sheets in his 23 appearances this season in Primera Division RFEF Group 2.

In Atlético Baleares's net, Ramón Vila Rovira has five clean sheets in 14 games. He has conceded a goal every 61 minutes, 70% more often than the 104 minutes between goals for López Galisteo.

Ceuta's last match was on April 14, a 4-0 win against Linares Deportivo, with Julio Iglesias Rojas getting the goals for Ceuta.

Atlético Baleares lost 1-0 against UD Ibiza last time out, also on April 14.
