UD Ibiza v CD Alcoyano

Primera Division RFEF Group 2
Sunday, 21 January 2024 @ 11.00am

Two of Primera Division RFEF Group 2's meanest defences go head-to-head on Sunday, when UD Ibiza host CD Alcoyano.

No teams have conceded fewer goals than Ibiza to date: the home side have let in just 16 goals in 19 games.

Alcoyano have conceded 17 goals in 19 games, giving them the joint-third tightest back line so far this season.

Key to Ibiza's home form has been Patrick Gilmar Sequeira Mejías, who has allowed two goals past him per 90 minutes, compared to 1 for José Perales Nájera in the opposite net.

Ibiza are second in the table after 19 games, of which they have won 13 and drawn five, earning 44 points.

Alcoyano are 10 places behind the home side in 12th, with seven wins and four draws putting them on 25 points.

Ibiza are in good form in Primera Division RFEF Group 2, with four wins and two draws from their last six games.

With three wins and three losses over that period, the visitors' form is worse – they have taken nine points from 18, compared to Ibiza's 14.

Ibiza's last match was on January 13, a 1-1 draw against Real Madrid B, with Alberto Escassi Oliva getting the goal for Ibiza.

Alcoyano beat Antequera CF 2-1 last time out, on Sunday, with Adrián Armental González and Raúl Toni Alcaina Fenollosa on the scoresheet.
