UD Melilla v Mérida AD

Primera Division RFEF Group 2 | Estadio Álvarez Claro
Sunday, 21 April 2024 @ 11.00am

UD Melilla host Mérida AD on Sunday at the Estadio Álvarez Claro in Primera Division RFEF Group 2.

In their last league match, on April 14, Melilla lost to Real Murcia 2-0 away.

Mérida AD won, 3-1 at home against Algeciras CF on April 13, with Rafael González Rodríguez (two) and Iñaki Elejalde Rodríguez on the scoresheet.

Melilla are 18th in the table after 32 games, of which they have won six and drawn six, earning 24 points.

Mérida AD are four places ahead of the hosts in 14th, with 10 wins and eight draws putting them on 38 points.

In the last 10 years, Melilla and Mérida AD have played each other on nine occasions. They won four each, and they drew once.

On average, Melilla scored 1.3 goals and Mérida AD 0.9 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 10, when Mérida AD won 2-0 at home.

Melilla are in disappointing form in Primera Division RFEF Group 2, with one win and two draws from their last six games.

With three wins and a draw over that period, the away side's form is better – they have taken 10 points from 18, compared to Melilla's five.

With 21 goals in 32 games so far this season, the home team are the league's third-lowest scorers with 0.7 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 48 goals at a rate of 1.5 per game.

Mérida AD are also below average scorers, with 0.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.1. They have conceded 1.2 goals per game.
