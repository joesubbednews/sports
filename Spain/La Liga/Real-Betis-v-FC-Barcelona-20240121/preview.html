Real Betis v FC Barcelona

La Liga | Estadio Benito Villamarín
Sunday, 21 January 2024 @ 5.30pm

Real Betis face FC Barcelona at the Estadio Benito Villamarín on late Sunday looking to secure a first win in six La Liga games.

Betis have lost one and drawn four matches since they last earned three points – against UD Las Palmas on November 26.

They face a Barcelona side who have won three and drawn one over that time.

With 36 goals in 19 games so far this season, Barcelona are scoring more than average in the league with 1.9 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 22 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

Betis, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have also conceded 1.1 goals per game.

Barça are third in the table after 19 games, of which they have won 12 and drawn five, earning 41 points.

The hosts are four places behind the away team in seventh, with six wins and 10 draws putting them on 28 points.

In the last 10 years, Betis and Barcelona have played each other on 19 occasions. Betis won two of them, Barcelona 15, and they drew twice.

On average, Betis scored 1.2 goals and Barcelona 3.2 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 16, when Barcelona won 5-0 at home.

With Marc-André ter Stegen between the sticks, Barcelona can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept six clean sheets in his 13 appearances this season in La Liga.

In Betis's net, Rui Silva has four clean sheets in 10 games. He has conceded a goal every 86 minutes, 10% more often than the 98 minutes between goals for ter Stegen.

Betis's last match was on January 3, a 2-1 loss against Celta de Vigo, with Aitor Ruibal getting the goal for Betis.

Barcelona beat UD Las Palmas 2-1 last time out, on January 4, with Ferrán Torres and İlkay Gündoğan on the scoresheet.
