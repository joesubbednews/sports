Real Madrid v FC Barcelona

La Liga | Estadio Santiago Bernabéu
Sunday, 21 April 2024 @ 8.00pm

La Liga's top two sides face each other at the Estadio Santiago Bernabéu in Sunday's late kick-off, when Real Madrid host second-placed FC Barcelona.

Madrid have picked up 24 wins and six draws from 31 games so far this season, and sit eight points above the visitors going into the 8pm match.

Barcelona, meanwhile, have won 21 and drawn seven, picking up 70 points.

With 67 goals in 31 games so far this season, Madrid are the league's highest scorers with 2.2 goals per game. And they are conceding fewer than average, letting in 20 goals at a rate of 0.6 per game.

Barcelona are also above average scorers, with 2.0 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.1 goals per game.

In Jude Bellingham, the home team have one of the league's sharpest shooters so far this season. He has notched 16 goals in 24 appearances, to sit second in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 127 minutes is quicker than that of Robert Lewandowski, Barça's top scorer with a goal every 172 minutes, and a total of 13 goals in 28 games.

Madrid are in fantastic form in La Liga, with five wins and a draw from their last six games.

And also with five wins and a draw over that period, the visitors' form is identical – they have both taken 16 points from 18.

In the last 10 years, Madrid and Barcelona have played each other on 31 occasions. Madrid won 13 of them, Barcelona 14, and they drew four times.

On average, Madrid scored 1.5 goals and Barcelona 1.7 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on January 14, when Madrid won 4-1 at home.

Madrid's last match was on April 13, a 1-0 win against RCD Mallorca, with Aurélien Tchouaméni getting the goal for Madrid.

Barcelona beat Cádiz 1-0 last time out, also on April 13, with João Félix on the scoresheet.
