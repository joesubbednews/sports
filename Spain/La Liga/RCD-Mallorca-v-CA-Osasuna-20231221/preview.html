RCD Mallorca v CA Osasuna

La Liga | Estadi Mallorca Son Moix
Thursday, 21 December 2023 @ 8.30pm

CA Osasuna travel to RCD Mallorca on late Thursday in La Liga.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a win in their last match, having beaten Rayo Vallecano 1-0 at home, with a goal from Raúl García de Haro.

RCD Mallorca, meanwhile, drew their last match, 0-0 against Almería.

In the last 10 years, Mallorca and Osasuna have played each other on 12 occasions. Mallorca won three of them, Osasuna five, and they drew four times.

On average, RCD Mallorca scored 1.5 goals and CA Osasuna 1.6 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on March 31, when they played out a 0-0 draw.

In Ante Budimir, Osasuna have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched eight goals in 17 appearances, to sit fourth in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 153 minutes is slightly quicker than that of Abdón Prats, Mallorca's top scorer with a goal every 127 minutes, and a total of five goals in 14 games.

With 14 goals in 17 games so far this season, RCD Mallorca are the league's joint-third-lowest scorers with 0.8 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 20 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

CA Osasuna are also below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.5 goals per game.

The hosts are 15th in the table after 17 games, of which they have won two and drawn nine, earning 15 points.

The away side are three places ahead of Mallorca in 12th, with five wins and four draws putting them on 19 points.

Mallorca are in disappointing form in La Liga, with one win and three draws from their last six games.

And also with a win and three draws over that period, Osasuna's form is identical – they have both taken six points from 18.
