Girona v Rayo Vallecano

La Liga | Estadi Municipal de Montilivi
Monday, 26 February 2024 @ 8.00pm

Girona host Rayo Vallecano in Monday's late match at Estadi Municipal de Montilivi looking to bounce back from defeat last time out in La Liga.

Girona, who sit second in the league after 25 games, fell to a 3-2 away defeat to Athletic Club Bilbao on February 19.

They face a Rayo side who secured a draw in their last match, a 1-1 tie with Real Madrid, and who sit 14th in the table.

With 54 goals in 25 games so far this season, Girona are the league's highest scorers with 2.2 goals per game. And they are conceding at an average rate, letting in 32 goals at a rate of 1.3 per game.

Rayo, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 0.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.3. They have conceded 1.3 goals per game.

The home team are in mixed form in La Liga, with two wins and two draws from their last six games.

With no wins and two draws over that period, the visitors' form is much worse – they have taken two points from 18, compared to Girona's eight.

In the last 10 years, Girona and Rayo have played each other on 15 occasions. They won six each, and they drew three times.

On average, Girona scored 1.4 goals and Rayo 1.3 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on January 17, when Girona won 3-1 at home.

In Artem Dovbyk, Girona have one of the league's most on-form strikers so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 23 appearances, to sit third in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 108 minutes is much quicker than that of Álvaro García, Rayo's top scorer with a goal every 300 minutes, and a total of six goals in 22 games.

Monday's match will be refereed by Ricardo De Burgos Bengoetxea, who has taken charge of 12 La Liga games so far this season, issuing two red cards and booking 44 players. He has awarded one penalty.

The last Girona game De Burgos Bengoetxea refereed was a 1-1 away draw with Real Betis on December 21. He is yet to oversee a match featuring Rayo this season.
