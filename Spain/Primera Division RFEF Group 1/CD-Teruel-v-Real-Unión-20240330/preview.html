CD Teruel v Real Unión

Primera Division RFEF Group 1 | Estadio La Pinila
Saturday, 30 March 2024 @ 5.00pm

Real Unión travel to the Estadio La Pinila looking to secure a first win in eight Primera Division RFEF Group 1 games against CD Teruel on late Saturday.

Unión have lost three and drawn four matches since they last earned three points – against SD Tarazona on January 27.

They face a Teruel side who have won three and drawn two over that time.

With 21 goals in 28 games so far this season, Teruel are scoring at below the league average rate with 0.8 goals per game. And they are conceding at an average rate, letting in 28 goals at a rate of 1.0 per game.

Unión, meanwhile, are above average scorers, with 1.2 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.0. They have conceded 1.4 goals per game.

In Alberto Solís Gómez, the visitors have one of the league's sharpest shooters so far this season. He has notched eight goals in 24 appearances, to sit second in the scoring charts.

The home side's top scorers, with two goals each, are Daniel Villanueva Rivas and Borja Martínez Giner.

Teruel are 17th in the table after 28 games, of which they have won four and drawn 15, earning 27 points.

Unión are three places ahead of Teruel in 14th, with eight wins and eight draws putting them on 32 points.

Teruel's last match was on March 16, a 4-2 loss against Barcelona B, with Aitor Pascual Sejias getting the goals for Teruel.

Unión lost 3-1 against Real Sociedad B last time out, on March 17, with Iván Pérez Cuevas on the scoresheet.
