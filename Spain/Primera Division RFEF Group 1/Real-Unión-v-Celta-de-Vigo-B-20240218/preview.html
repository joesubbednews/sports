Real Unión v Celta de Vigo B

Primera Division RFEF Group 1 | Estadio Gal
Sunday, 18 February 2024 @ 11.00am

Sunday's early match between Real Unión and Celta de Vigo B promises to be one for the neutrals, as two of Primera Division RFEF Group 1's most free-scoring sides go head-to-head.

Ahead of the game at the Estadio Gal, Unión and Celta sit joint-second and top in the goal-scoring charts, with 30 and 36 goals respectively.

Iván Pérez Cuevas and Alejandro Cerdá Agulló lead the way for the home side, having bagged one goal each in their 22 games to date.

And Pablo Durán Fernández has been the main man in the opponents' penalty box for Celta, with one goal.

Celta are fifth in the table after 22 games, of which they have won 12 and drawn two, earning 38 points.

Unión are six places behind the away team in 11th, with eight wins and five draws putting them on 29 points.

The hosts are in mixed form in Primera Division RFEF Group 1, with two wins and three draws from their last six games.

With two wins and four losses over that period, Celta's form is worse – they have taken six points from 18, compared to Unión's nine.

In the last three years, Unión and Celta have played each other on three occasions. Unión won one of them and Celta the other.

Their last meeting was on October 14, when Celta won 3-0 at home.

Unión's last match was on February 3, a 0-0 draw against CD Lugo.

Celta lost 2-1 against UE Cornellà last time out, also on February 3, with Hugo Álvarez Antúnez on the scoresheet.
