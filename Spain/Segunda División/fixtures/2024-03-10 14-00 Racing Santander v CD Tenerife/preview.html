Racing Santander v CD Tenerife

Segunda División | Campos de Sport de El Sardinero
Sunday, 10 March 2024 @ 1.00pm

CD Tenerife travel to Racing Santander on Sunday lunchtime in the Segunda División.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a win in their last match, having beaten Mirandés 2-1 at home, with goals from Enric Gallego Puigsech and Luis Miguel Cruz Hernández.

Racing, meanwhile, drew their last match, 2-2 against Racing Club de Ferrol, with their goals scored by Rubén González Alves and Gerard Fernández Castellano.

With 44 goals in 29 games so far this season, Racing are the league's third-highest scorers with 1.5 goals per game. But they are conceding more than average too, letting in 41 goals at a rate of 1.4 per game.

Tenerife, meanwhile, are below average scorers, with 0.9 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.1. They have also conceded 0.9 goals per game.

In Fernández Castellano, the home side have one of the league's sharpest shooters so far this season. He has notched 14 goals in 28 appearances, to sit second in the scoring charts.

His goal rate of one every 140 minutes is quicker than that of Ángel Rodríguez, the visitors' top scorer with a goal every 170 minutes, and a total of seven goals in 24 games.

In the last 10 years, Racing and Tenerife have played each other on seven occasions. Racing won one of them, Tenerife four, and they drew twice.

On average, Racing scored 0.9 goals and Tenerife 1.4 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 3, when Tenerife won 2-0 at home.

Racing are 10th in the table after 29 games, of which they have won 12 and drawn seven, earning 43 points.

Tenerife are two places behind Racing in 12th, with 11 wins and seven draws putting them on 40 points.

The hosts are in mixed form in the Segunda División, with three wins and a draw from their last six games.

With two wins and three draws over that period, Tenerife's form is slightly worse – they have taken nine points from 18, compared to Racing's 10.

Sunday's match will be refereed by Oliver De La Fuente Ramos, who has taken charge of 14 Segunda División games so far this season, issuing four red cards and booking 66 players. He has awarded five penalties.

The last Racing game De La Fuente Ramos refereed was a 3-3 away draw with CD Eldense on December 16. He is yet to oversee a match featuring Tenerife this season.
