Burgos CF v Sporting Gijón

Segunda División | Estadio Municipal de El Plantío
Sunday, 25 February 2024 @ 3.15pm

Sporting Gijón travel to Burgos CF on Sunday in the Segunda División.

The visitors come into the game on the back of a win in their last match, having beaten Real Oviedo 1-0 at home, with a goal from Ignacio Méndez Navia Fernández.

Burgos also won their last match, 2-0 against Racing Club de Ferrol, with their goals scored by Daniel Ojeda Saranova and Borja González Tejada.

In Orlando Rubén Yáñez Alabart, Sporting can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept 11 clean sheets in his 25 appearances this season, and no 'keeper has prevented the opposition scoring more often in the Segunda División.

In Burgos's net, José Antonio Caro Díaz has 10 clean sheets in 26 games. He has conceded a goal every 81 minutes, 30% more often than the 107 minutes between goals for Yáñez Alabart.

With 34 goals in 26 games so far this season, the hosts are scoring more than average in the league with 1.3 goals per game. But they are conceding more than average too, letting in 32 goals at a rate of 1.2 per game.

The away side are also above average scorers, with 1.2 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.1. They have conceded 0.8 goals per game.

Sporting are third in the table after 26 games, of which they have won 11 and drawn nine, earning 42 points.

Burgos are six places behind Sporting in ninth, with 11 wins and six draws putting them on 39 points.

In the last 10 years, Burgos and Sporting have played each other on six occasions. Sporting won two of them and they drew four times.

On average, Burgos scored 0.3 goals and Sporting 0.7 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on September 2, when Sporting won 2-1 at home.

Burgos are in mixed form in the Segunda División, with three wins and a draw from their last six games.

With two wins and two draws over that period, the visitors' form is slightly worse – they have taken eight points from 18, compared to the home team's 10.
