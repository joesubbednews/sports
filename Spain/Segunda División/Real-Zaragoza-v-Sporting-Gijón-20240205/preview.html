Real Zaragoza v Sporting Gijón

Segunda División | Estadio de la Romareda
Monday, 5 February 2024 @ 7.30pm

Two of the Segunda División's meanest defences go head-to-head at Estadio de la Romareda on Monday, when Sporting Gijón visit Real Zaragoza.

Only one side – Leganés – has conceded fewer goals than Sporting to date: the away side have let in just 19 goals in 24 games.

Zaragoza have conceded 22 goals in 24 games, giving them the joint-fourth tightest back line so far this season.

Key to Sporting's home form has been Orlando Rubén Yáñez Alabart, who has allowed 0.75 goals past him per 90 minutes, compared to 0.62 for Cristian Darío Álvarez in the opposite net.

In the last 10 years, Zaragoza and Sporting have played each other on 16 occasions. Zaragoza won eight of them, Sporting four, and they drew four times.

On average, Zaragoza scored 1.3 goals and Sporting 1.1 in those matches.

Their last meeting was on October 14, when they played out a 2-2 draw.

Sporting's José Ángel is among the league's most creative players, racking up six assists in 22 appearances so far this season, and holding second spot in the Segunda División's assist charts.

For Zaragoza, Francisco Gámez López has set up the most goals, having laid on four assists in 19 games.

The visitors are seventh in the table after 24 games, of which they have won 10 and drawn nine, earning 39 points.

The home side are seven places behind Sporting in 14th, with eight wins and nine draws putting them on 33 points.

Zaragoza are in mixed form in the Segunda División, with one win and five draws from their last six games.

With a win and four draws over that period, Sporting's form is slightly worse – they have taken seven points from 18, compared to Zaragoza's eight.

Zaragoza's last match was on January 27, a 0-0 draw against AD Alcorcón.

Sporting lost 2-1 against Racing Club de Ferrol last time out, on January 28, with Carlos Roberto Izquierdoz on the scoresheet.

Monday's match will be refereed by Saúl Ais Reig, who has taken charge of 12 Segunda División games so far this season, issuing one red card and booking 55 players. He has awarded three penalties.

The last Zaragoza game Ais Reig refereed was a 3-1 away win against FC Cartagena on September 10. He is yet to oversee a match featuring Sporting this season.
