SD Huesca v SD Amorebieta

Segunda División | Estadio El Alcoraz
Sunday, 18 February 2024 @ 5.30pm

Struggling SD Amorebieta face SD Huesca away at the Estadio El Alcoraz on Sunday looking to build on a win in their last league outing.

After securing all three points with a 1-0 victory over Elche CF on February 10, Amorebieta sit bottom of the Segunda División.

They travel to play a Huesca side 16th in the standings, who were held in their last match, 0-0 against CD Eldense.

Huesca are in mixed form in the Segunda División, with two wins and three draws from their last six games.

With a win and a draw over that period, Amorebieta's form is worse – they have taken four points from 18, compared to the hosts' nine.

In Álvaro Fernández, Huesca can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept 10 clean sheets in his 26 appearances this season in the Segunda División.

In the away team's net, Pablo Cuñat Campos has five clean sheets in 22 games. He has conceded a goal every 79 minutes, 50% more often than the 117 minutes between goals for Fernández Llorente.

In the last three years, Huesca and Amorebieta have played each other on three occasions. They won one each, and they drew once.

Their last meeting was on November 5, when Huesca won 1-0 away.

With 20 goals in 26 games so far this season, Huesca are the league's joint-second-lowest scorers with 0.8 goals per game. But they are conceding fewer than average too, letting in 22 goals at a rate of 0.8 per game.

Amorebieta are also below average scorers, with 0.8 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.1. They have conceded 1.4 goals per game.

Sunday's match will be refereed by Rafael Sánchez López, who has taken charge of 13 Segunda División games so far this season, issuing two red cards and booking 50 players. He has awarded seven penalties.

The last Huesca game Sánchez López refereed was a 0-0 home draw with Albacete Balompié on October 29. His last Amorebieta match was their 2-1 loss away at Real Valladolid on December 9.
