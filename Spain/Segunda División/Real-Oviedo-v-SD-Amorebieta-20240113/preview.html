Real Oviedo v SD Amorebieta

Segunda División | Estadio Nuevo Carlos Tartiere
Saturday, 13 January 2024 @ 5.30pm

SD Amorebieta travel to the Estadio Nuevo Carlos Tartiere looking to secure a first win in six Segunda División games against Real Oviedo on late Saturday.

SD Amorebieta have lost four and drawn one matches since they last earned three points – against CD Tenerife on November 18.

They face an Oviedo side who have won two and drawn two over that time.

Amorebieta are 21st in the table after 21 games, of which they have won three and drawn six, earning 15 points.

Oviedo are 11 places ahead of SD Amorebieta in 10th, with seven wins and nine draws putting them on 30 points.

With Leo Román between the sticks, Real Oviedo can rely on one of the league's safest pair of hands. He has kept eight clean sheets in his 21 appearances this season, and only two other 'keepers – Sporting Gijón's Orlando Rubén Yáñez Alabart and CD Tenerife's Juan Soriano Oropesa – have been able to prevent the opposition scoring on more occasions in the Segunda División.

In the away team's net, Pablo Cuñat Campos has four clean sheets in 17 games. He has conceded a goal every 77 minutes, 60% more often than the 126 minutes between goals for Román Riquelme.

With 18 goals in 21 games so far this season, Amorebieta are the league's third-lowest scorers with 0.9 goals per game. And they are conceding more than average, letting in 31 goals at a rate of 1.5 per game.

The hosts are also below average scorers, with 1.1 goals per game, compared to a league average of 1.2. They have conceded 0.8 goals per game.

Over the last two years, Oviedo and Amorebieta have played each other twice. they drew both of them.

Their last meeting was on October 21, when they played out a 0-0 draw.

Oviedo's last match was on December 21, a 1-1 draw against Villarreal B, with Alejandro Suárez Cardero getting the goal for Real Oviedo.

Amorebieta lost 2-1 against AD Alcorcón last time out, also on December 21, with Félix Garreta on the scoresheet.
